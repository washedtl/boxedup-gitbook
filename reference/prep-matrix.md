# Prep matrix (by category)

🟢 **Reference.** Quick-lookup table for FBA prep requirements by category. Bookmark this page.

> ⚠️ Amazon updates prep requirements periodically. Verify with Amazon's category-specific guidelines for any high-stakes shipments.

## Universal requirements

These apply to every FBA unit, regardless of category:

- ✅ **FNSKU label** on a flat surface, scannable
- ✅ **Packaging integrity** — items survive transit
- ✅ **Quantity match** — what's in the box matches your shipment plan
- ✅ **Cover original UPC** OR ensure FNSKU is dominant (prevents commingling)
- ✅ **No retailer-branded packaging** if shipping items meant for other retailers (cover up "Walmart Exclusive" labels, etc.)

## Category-specific prep

### Apparel & Accessories
- ✅ **Polybag mandatory** (suffocation warning if opening ≥5")
- ✅ **Minimum 1.5 mil polybag thickness**
- ✅ Tags must be intact
- ⚠️ Used apparel: Amazon's authenticity bar is high; risky
- ⚠️ Apparel returns: high (15–30%); plan accordingly

### Beauty
- ✅ Polybag if liquid or could leak
- ✅ Tape lid if leak risk
- ✅ Expiration date visible
- ⚠️ Most beauty requires category ungating
- ⚠️ Some beauty considered hazmat (aerosols, certain solvents)

### Books / Media
- ✅ Just FNSKU; no special prep
- ✅ Used books: condition-grade carefully
- ⚠️ Lower margin category; high-volume play

### Electronics
- ✅ Original packaging if possible
- ✅ Bubble wrap if fragile
- ✅ Polybag if exposed components
- ⚠️ Lithium batteries: hazmat approval required
- ⚠️ Pre-owned electronics: serial verification critical (counterfeit risk)

### Food / Grocery
- ✅ Expiration date visible
- ✅ Polybag if leak risk
- ✅ FNSKU on outer packaging
- ⚠️ Category requires ungating
- ⚠️ Perishable: extra storage rules
- ⚠️ Many grocery items are hazmat (pressurized, etc.)

### Hazmat
- ⚠️ **REQUIRES hazmat approval before listing** (1–6 weeks)
- ⚠️ MSDS (Material Safety Data Sheet) submission required
- ✅ Special inbound prep per item
- ✅ Restricted shipping methods

Hazmat includes:
- Lithium batteries (most laptops, phones, power banks)
- Aerosols (most spray products)
- Flammables (some cleaning products, alcohol-based)
- Magnetized items (some toys, electronics)

### Home Goods
- ✅ Polybag for soft goods (towels, bedding)
- ✅ Bubble wrap for fragile (glassware, ceramics)
- ✅ Box reinforcement for sets
- ⚠️ Oversize fees for large kitchen appliances

### Jewelry
- ✅ Original case + tags
- ✅ Bubble wrap for fragile pieces
- ⚠️ High counterfeit risk (especially gold/silver)
- ⚠️ Most jewelry requires category ungating

### Office Supplies
- ✅ Polybag if loose components
- ✅ Standard FNSKU
- ⚠️ Low margin generally; high-volume play

### Pet Supplies
- ✅ Polybag if multi-component (toys, training aids)
- ✅ Expiration date for treats / food
- ⚠️ Generally good category for arbitrage

### Personal Care
- ✅ Polybag for liquids
- ✅ Suffocation warning if applicable
- ⚠️ Often gated; check before sourcing

### Pharmacy / Supplements
- ⚠️ Often restricted entirely
- ⚠️ Expiration dates strictly enforced
- ⚠️ FDA compliance issues
- ⚠️ Skip unless you're licensed pharmacy

### Sports & Outdoors
- ✅ Polybag soft goods
- ✅ Original packaging for equipment
- ✅ Bubble wrap fragile parts
- ⚠️ Camping/sporting equipment often oversize

### Toys & Games
- ✅ Polybag soft toys (always)
- ✅ Polybag toys with loose components
- ✅ Suffocation warning for ≥5" openings
- ✅ "Sold as set" sticker for multi-piece sets
- ⚠️ Q4 toy ungating window
- ⚠️ Brand IP risk (Disney, Nintendo, LEGO without invoice)

### Video Games
- ✅ Original case if possible
- ✅ Bubble wrap fragile (newer-gen disc-based)
- ⚠️ Used video games: condition grading critical
- ⚠️ Pre-owned has different listing requirements

### Watches
- ✅ Original case + tags
- ✅ Bubble wrap
- ⚠️ Very high counterfeit risk
- ⚠️ Category often requires ungating

## Polybag requirements detail

For any polybag-required category:

### Bag specs
- ✅ Transparent or translucent (so UPC/FNSKU visible from outside)
- ✅ Minimum 1.5 mil thickness
- ✅ Sealed (heat-seal, self-adhesive, or zip-top with secondary seal)
- ✅ Within 0.5" of unit dimensions (no excess flapping)

### Suffocation warning
Required when polybag opening is ≥5". Required text:

```
WARNING: To avoid danger of suffocation, keep this bag away from
babies and children. Do not use this bag in cribs, beds, carriages,
or playpens. This bag is not a toy.
```

Buy:
- Pre-printed suffocation polybags (Amazon, Uline, Veritiv)
- Standalone suffocation labels

## Bubble wrap guidelines

### When to bubble wrap
- ✅ Glass / ceramic / porcelain
- ✅ Mirrors
- ✅ Fragile electronics (LCDs, sensors)
- ✅ Anything where damage risk is real

### Bubble wrap specs
- 1/2" or larger bubbles for high-fragility
- Wrap completely (no exposed corners)
- Tape secure but removable

### When NOT to bubble wrap
- Items in original retail boxes (the box is the protection)
- Sturdy items (most plastic toys, books)
- Items where bubble adds excessive weight (cost increase)

## FNSKU label requirements

### Position
- ✅ On a flat surface (not curved seam)
- ✅ Cover original UPC (not strictly required but reduces commingling)
- ✅ On the outermost packaging (the box, not the inner item)

### Material
- ✅ Thermal label paper (DYMO 30334, 30330)
- ✅ Adhesive sticks well, doesn't peel

### Print quality
- ✅ Crisp, scannable (not blurry)
- ✅ Sufficient white space around barcode
- ❌ Not on regular printer paper (won't survive transit)

## Cover-up labeling

When original packaging mentions another retailer (Walmart Exclusive, etc.):

### What to cover
- ✅ Retailer-specific stickers
- ✅ Retailer-specific UPC if different from listing UPC
- ✅ "Made for [retailer]" text in some cases

### How to cover
- Plain white sticker (Avery 5160 / similar)
- Apply cleanly, no overlap with FNSKU
- Don't cover important product info (warnings, ingredients)

## Set / bundle prep

For multi-piece sets:

### Physical bundling
- ✅ Items physically connected (taped, polybagged, sealed inner box)
- ✅ Cannot be disassembled in customer's hand without effort

### Labeling
- ✅ "Sold as set, do not separate" sticker on outside
- ✅ FNSKU on outside set (not on individual pieces)

### Without "Sold as set" sticker
Warehouse workers may split sets into individual units. You'll have phantom inventory issues.

## Hazmat prep checklist

For hazmat items (after pre-approval):

### Before listing
1. ✅ MSDS submission to Amazon
2. ✅ Hazmat review approval received
3. ✅ Listing flagged as hazmat in Seller Central

### Inbound prep
1. ✅ Sealed in original packaging (can't repack)
2. ✅ No leaks
3. ✅ Hazmat warning labels intact
4. ✅ Ship via Amazon-approved carriers + methods

### Different SKU/FNSKU treatment
Hazmat units may have hazmat-specific FNSKUs. Use the right one for the hazmat-classified ASIN.

## Prep service decision

Should you outsource prep?

| Volume | DIY | Prep service |
|---|---|---|
| <50 units/month | DIY | Probably overkill |
| 50–200 | DIY usually | Consider for time-constrained months |
| 200–500 | Mixed | Outsource non-replens |
| 500+ | Outsource | Definitely |

Cost: \$0.50–\$1.50/unit + monthly minimum (\$500–\$2,000).

Find prep services via:
- Prep Centers Network (directory)
- Discord recommendations
- Reseller-focused 3PL search

## When prep fails

Amazon's response to prep violations:

- **Minor (missing FNSKU):** Amazon labels for you, charges \$0.30/unit
- **Moderate (no polybag on required item):** warning + chargeback
- **Major (no suffocation warning):** \$0.30–\$1.00/unit prep fee + delay
- **Severe (hazmat without approval):** listing suspension + possible account action
- **Repeat offender:** Amazon disables your "no prep" SKU options globally

After your first chargeback, you'll never skip prep again.
