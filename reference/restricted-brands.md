# Restricted brands master list

🟡 **Reference.** Quick-lookup of brands that require special handling. The lists shift over time — verify current status before sourcing.

> ⚠️ **This list is illustrative, not exhaustive.** Always verify gating + IP risk per ASIN with your scanner before sourcing.

## Tier 1: Aggressive enforcement, AVOID

These brands have active brand-protection teams that file IP/inauthentic claims aggressively. Even with retail receipts, sourcing carries severe risk.

### Brands to skip without authorized invoices

- **Apple** (any product, any accessory)
- **Beats by Dre**
- **Disney** (any character: Frozen, Mickey, Star Wars, Marvel, Pixar)
- **Pixar** (often filed with Disney)
- **Marvel** (any character)
- **Star Wars** (any product)
- **Nintendo** (Mario, Zelda, Pokemon, Switch, etc.)
- **Pokemon** (cards, plush, anything)
- **LEGO** (without LEGO ungating)
- **Sony** (premium electronics)
- **Bose** (audio)

### Apparel / fashion brands to skip

- **Nike** (consistent IP enforcement)
- **Adidas**
- **Under Armour**
- **Lululemon**

### Lifestyle brands hot-list

- **Stanley** (the cups)
- **Hydro Flask**
- **Yeti** (selectively)
- **Owala**

### Luxury (NEVER)

- **Chanel, Gucci, Louis Vuitton, Prada, Dior** — any luxury brand
- **Rolex, Omega, Patek Philippe** — luxury watches
- Almost any designer-named product

## Tier 2: Moderate enforcement

File complaints occasionally but appeals win with proper documentation. Source with care.

- **Crocs**
- **Calvin Klein**
- **Tommy Hilfiger**
- **Ralph Lauren** (mid-tier)
- **L'Oréal** (some product lines)
- **Olaplex**
- **Drunk Elephant**
- **Glossier**
- **The Ordinary**
- **Most premium beauty**
- **Dyson**
- **KitchenAid**

For these:
- Save receipts
- Limit volume per SKU (don't be the seller with 200 units)
- Get wholesale invoices when possible

## Tier 3: Permissive

These brands rarely file. RA + OA receipts usually defend successfully.

- Most CPG (Procter & Gamble, Unilever, Pepsi, Coca-Cola)
- Most generic toys (Hot Wheels, Hasbro core, Mattel mid-tier)
- Most small/mid-tier apparel
- Most niche / small brands
- Mid-tier kitchen brands (OXO, Lodge, etc.)
- Most pet supply brands
- Most office supplies

You should still:
- Save receipts
- Don't source obviously stolen-looking inventory
- Avoid private-label conflicts

## Q4 toy gating (annual)

Toys & Games sometimes adds Q4 restrictions for new sellers:

### Approximate threshold (varies year to year)
- 25+ orders shipped from Sep 1 onward
- ODR < 1%
- Late shipment rate < 4%
- Cancellation rate < 1.75%

If below threshold, locked out of new toy ASINs Oct–Dec.

**Plan:** ramp orders in August/September if you intend to play Q4 toys.

## Category gating (separate from brand)

Some categories require category-level approval:

| Category | Typical requirement |
|---|---|
| **Beauty** | Application + sometimes wholesale invoice |
| **Topical Health** | Application + medical claims compliance |
| **Health & Personal Care** | Application + sometimes ungating |
| **Pharmacy** | Heavy regulation, often closed |
| **Grocery & Gourmet Food** | Application + invoice often |
| **Watches** | Application |
| **Jewelry** | Application |
| **Automotive (some sub-cat)** | Application |
| **Hazmat** | MSDS + approval per item |

If a listing requires category approval and you haven't applied yet, you can't sell — even if eligible for the brand.

## Restricted product categories

Some categories Amazon won't let you sell at all (or only with extensive documentation):

- **Weapons / firearms** — generally banned
- **Tobacco products** — banned
- **Explosives + ammunition** — banned
- **Drugs / controlled substances** — banned
- **Alcohol** — heavily restricted
- **Lethal medical devices** — banned

Check Seller Central → Sell on Amazon → "What can I sell" for current rules.

## Brand-protection methods

How brands enforce their listings:

### Brand Registry tools
- Removal of unauthorized sellers
- IP complaint filing
- Listing control

### Test buys
- Brand buys from your offer
- Tests for authenticity
- Files counterfeit complaint if confirmed fake

### Project Zero (large brands)
- Direct removal of counterfeit listings
- No test-buy required
- Reserved for high-volume IP holders

### Transparency Program
- Anti-counterfeit codes per unit
- Customers verify authenticity
- Counterfeit reports = 1-click

If a brand uses Transparency, you cannot sell their products without the codes (i.e., you must source from authorized channels).

## Self-defense protocols

Even with retail receipts, your defense isn't bulletproof against aggressive brands. Best practices:

### For Tier 2 brands (moderate enforcement)
- Save every receipt photographed
- Source from major retailers only (Walmart, Target, etc.)
- Limit per-SKU inventory commitment
- Build wholesale invoice path within 90 days

### For Tier 1 brands (aggressive)
- Wholesale invoices required to source safely
- Without invoices: avoid the brand entirely
- Even with invoices: be prepared for sporadic complaints

### Universal
- Don't source obvious counterfeits
- Don't commingle inventory
- Maintain clean account health
- Respond to complaints within 24 hours

## When brand "tier" changes

Brands sometimes shift tiers:

### Tier 3 → Tier 2 (becoming more aggressive)
Brand registers + starts filing complaints. Watch for pattern: 2+ inauthentic complaints from same brand in 90 days.

**Action:** stop sourcing new inventory; sell through existing.

### Tier 2 → Tier 1 (escalating)
Brand expands their brand-protection team or hires a third-party enforcer. Pattern: 3+ complaints in 30 days, restored only after lengthy POAs.

**Action:** drop the brand entirely; build wholesale path or move on.

### Tier 1 → Tier 2 (relaxing, rare)
Sometimes a brand changes ownership or strategy. Watch for community signals.

**Action:** re-evaluate cautiously; small test batches first.

## Wholesale vs retail invoice

For most Tier 1 + Tier 2 brand defenses:

### Wholesale invoice
- ✅ From authorized brand distributor
- ✅ Brand acknowledges your business as buyer
- ✅ Lists specific quantities + items
- ✅ Wins inauthentic appeals

### Retail receipt
- ⚠️ From Walmart, Target, etc.
- ⚠️ Brand doesn't necessarily acknowledge channel
- ⚠️ May or may not win appeals depending on brand
- ⚠️ Limited defensive value for Tier 1 brands

For RA/OA-only sourcers: retail receipts are your only defense. For wholesale-graduated sourcers: invoices are bulletproof.

## When to ungate vs walk

For brands you want to source long-term:

### Ungate path
- Apply for brand authorization
- Provide wholesale invoice (sometimes)
- Build relationship with brand
- Receive brand-approved status
- Source freely going forward

### Walk path
- Brand requires letter of authorization that's hard to get
- Wholesale MOQ too high for your scale
- Brand has policy against new resellers
- Move on to other opportunities

For most BoxedUp members: ungate Tier 2 brands you sell often; walk from Tier 1 brands until established.
