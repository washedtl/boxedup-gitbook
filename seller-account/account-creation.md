# Account creation walkthrough

🟢 **Beginner.** Step-by-step for opening your Amazon seller account. Get this right and you'll save weeks of friction later.

## Before you click "Sign up"

Have these ready before starting:

- ✅ **Government-issued ID** — driver's license or passport
- ✅ **Business entity** — LLC paperwork or sole prop with EIN (better than SSN)
- ✅ **Business bank account** — needs to be in your name or LLC's name
- ✅ **Credit card** — used for monthly Pro fees + as backup; debit cards often fail verification
- ✅ **Phone number** — must be one you can answer for SMS verification calls
- ✅ **Quiet, well-lit room** — for the video verification call
- ✅ **Recent utility bill or bank statement** — proof of address, dated within 90 days

If any of these are missing, **stop and get them first.** Restarting account verification is painful.

## The signup flow

Visit **sellercentral.amazon.com** → "Sign up" → "Create your Amazon account."

### Step 1: Account type

You'll be asked: **Individual** or **Professional**.

- Pick **Professional** (\$39.99/month)
- Don't be tempted by "Individual" to save money — see [Pro vs Individual](pro-vs-individual.md) for why

### Step 2: Business information

Enter:
- **Legal business name** — exactly as it appears on your LLC paperwork
- **Business type** — LLC / Sole prop / Corporation / Partnership
- **State of registration** — where your LLC is filed
- **Federal tax ID** — your EIN (preferred) or SSN

> **Pro tip:** if you're a sole prop with EIN, enter EIN in the Tax ID field — your account looks more professional during reviews.

### Step 3: Beneficial owner / Officer information

For each beneficial owner (anyone with >25% ownership):
- Full legal name
- Date of birth
- Residential address
- Country of citizenship
- ID number (driver's license / passport)

The information must match your government ID exactly — typos will fail verification.

### Step 4: Billing information

- **Credit card** — for monthly Pro fees. Use your business card if possible.
- **Bank account** — for Amazon to deposit your sales revenue. Must be a US bank account in the business name (or your name if sole prop).

### Step 5: Store information

- **Store name** — your seller-facing brand. Can be changed later. Pick something professional.
- **Product type** — what you'll mostly sell. Pick the broadest fit; doesn't lock you in.

### Step 6: Tax interview

A short questionnaire about your tax classification. As a US LLC or sole prop:
- Federal tax classification: depends on entity (most resellers: LLC → "Disregarded entity" if single-member, else as elected)
- Sign electronically with your full legal name

## Identity verification

After submitting the application, Amazon emails you about identity verification. Three steps:

### 1. Document upload

You'll upload:
- Government-issued ID (front + back)
- Bank statement OR utility bill OR credit card statement (matching your registered address)
- Sometimes additional documents (depending on your country/business type)

**Tips:**
- Use **PDF** or high-res JPEG (>200 DPI)
- Make sure all 4 corners are visible
- No glare or shadows
- File names matter less than content

### 2. Phone verification

Amazon calls or texts a verification code to the phone number on file. Make sure you can pick up in real-time.

### 3. Video verification call

The hardest step for many. Amazon schedules a 5–15 minute video call with a verification rep. You'll:
- Show your ID on camera
- Confirm your business details verbally
- Sometimes answer questions about your sourcing/business plan

**How to nail the video call:**
- ✅ **Be in a quiet, well-lit room** (not a moving car, not at Starbucks)
- ✅ **Have your ID + bank statement ready** in physical form
- ✅ **Speak clearly, look at the camera** — they're checking it's really you
- ✅ **Answer simply** — "I plan to sell consumer goods sourced from retail and wholesale" is fine
- ❌ **Don't ramble** about your business model — keep it boring
- ❌ **Don't get nervous about technical questions** — they're not testing knowledge

After the call, expect 1–14 days for verification. You can sell during this period (your account will be in "limited" mode), but won't get deposits until verified.

## Common verification fails

### Address mismatch
Your driver's license says "123 Main St" but your bank statement says "123 Main Street." Amazon's auto-matcher rejects.
- **Fix:** Submit a utility bill that exactly matches one of the other documents

### Document age
Bank statement is 91 days old.
- **Fix:** Pull a fresh statement from your bank's online portal

### LLC name mismatch
You registered as "Acme Trading LLC" but typed "Acme Trading L.L.C." in signup.
- **Fix:** Open a case asking to update the legal name to match your formation docs

### Failed video call
You couldn't take the call when scheduled, or had connectivity issues.
- **Fix:** Reschedule via the link in your verification email — typically takes 3–7 days for a new slot

## Right after verification

Once your account is verified:

### Set your shipping settings
- Inventory → Shipping → Settings
- Set ship-from address (often your home for FBA inbound)
- Configure return address (for FBM returns; FBA-only sellers can use Amazon's defaults)

### Configure tax settings
- Settings → Account info → Tax information
- Submit any state-level tax permits you've registered for
- Most sellers leave this minimal post-MPF laws (see [Sales tax](../business-setup/sales-tax.md))

### Set 2-step verification
- Settings → Login Settings → Two-Step Verification
- **Enable this immediately.** Account hijacking is real.
- Use authenticator app (Authy, Google Authenticator), not SMS — SMS is hijackable.

### Update return settings
- Returns settings → enable Amazon's automated return rules
- For FBA-only sellers, this is automatic
- For FBM, configure return address + restocking fee policies

### Connect Amazon Business
- Sign up for an Amazon Business buying account using your business email
- Submit your reseller certificate for tax-exempt purchases
- This saves 6–10% on every sourcing buy from Amazon

## What "limited mode" means

While verifying, your account is in **limited mode**:
- ✅ You can list products
- ✅ You can ship to FBA
- ✅ Customers can buy
- ❌ Amazon holds your sales revenue (no deposits)
- ❌ Some categories may be restricted

Once verified, holds release and back-deposits flow within 7–14 days.

## Settings to change in the first week

A handful of defaults that hurt resellers:

### 1. Customer message auto-response
- Settings → Notifications
- Set up a polite auto-response for buyer messages: "Thanks for reaching out — I'll respond within 24 hours."
- Amazon counts unanswered messages within 24 hours against your account health

### 2. FBA inbound packaging preference
- Settings → Fulfillment by Amazon → Inbound settings
- Default: "Allow box content information via web form" — this is fine for beginners
- Past 50 SKUs/shipment, switch to "Box Content Information File" upload

### 3. Repricing settings
- Don't enable Amazon's automated repricer initially — it's aggressive and tanks Buy Box price
- Set manual prices to start; revisit at \$5K/month with a third-party repricer (Aura, RepricerExpress)

### 4. Currency display
- If selling on Amazon US: USD
- Settings → Marketplaces → confirm

### 5. Vacation mode
- Know where this lives (Settings → Account Info → Listing Status)
- You'll need it during family emergencies, vacations, etc.

## Marketplace selection

When verifying, Amazon may ask which marketplaces you want.

- **US (Amazon.com)** — default for North American sellers
- **Canada (Amazon.ca)** — easy add-on for US sellers; uses NARF (North America Remote Fulfillment) for cross-border
- **Mexico (Amazon.com.mx)** — also part of NARF
- **EU / UK / JP / AU** — separate seller accounts; complex tax compliance; usually skip unless you have a specific reason

For BoxedUp purposes, **start with US only**. Add Canada once you're profitable + scaling.

## Account-health protective mode

For your first 90 days, Amazon scrutinizes accounts heavily. Protect yourself:

- 🛡️ **Don't sell anything Brand Registered without invoices** — IP claims hit new accounts hardest
- 🛡️ **Respond to every buyer message within 12 hours** — late messages hurt new accounts more than old
- 🛡️ **Avoid hazmat / restricted categories** — approval delays will block you mid-launch
- 🛡️ **Ship to FBA only** (skip FBM initially) — FBM defects are killers for new accounts
- 🛡️ **Don't change settings frequently** — Amazon flags unstable accounts

Treat the first 90 days like a probationary period. After that, the account becomes harder to suspend.
