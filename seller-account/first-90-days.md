# Surviving your first 90 days

🟢 **Beginner.** Amazon scrutinizes new accounts heavily. The first 90 days set your account's reputation. Survive this period clean and the rest gets dramatically easier.

## Why the first 90 days matter

Amazon's algorithm treats new accounts as **probationary**:

* Suspensions are easier (fewer appeals win)
* IP complaints have higher impact (one strike = harsh)
* Restock limits are tighter (lower IPI cap)
* Customer-message defects weighted heavier
* Funds held longer (deposit reserves are higher)

After 90 days of clean operation, the account "graduates" — appeals win more often, restock limits relax, reserves drop.

## The 90-day game plan

### Days 1–14: Setup + first sale

Goals:

* ✅ Account verified
* ✅ Payment + bank set up
* ✅ Reseller cert obtained
* ✅ First 5 ASINs sourced (low-risk, well-established categories)
* ✅ First shipment built and shipped to FBA
* ✅ First sale received
* ✅ Get started on your first ungate
* ✅ Signing up for Keepa

### Days 15–30: First 10 sales

Goals:

* ✅ 5–10 sales completed
* ✅ Zero customer complaints
* ✅ Account Health: all green metrics
* ✅ Actively using and practicing Keepa

**Watch for:**

* Late shipment notifications (FBA-only sellers shouldn't see these, but FBM sellers do)
* Negative feedback (respond within 24 hours, even if just to acknowledge)
* A-to-Z claim filings (rare on FBA but possible)

### Days 31–60: Build patterns

Goals:

* ✅ 30+ orders shipped
* ✅ 10+ unique SKUs sold
* ✅ Build account feedback from your recent sales

**Try:**

* Ungating more brands or categories
* Setting up SellerBoard for proper P\&L visibility

### Days 61–90: Scale prep

Goals:

* ✅ Craft a consistent flow between purchasing inventory and selling
* ✅ Comfortable with the FBA shipment workflow
* ✅ At least 1 or 2 brands ungated.
* ✅ Comfortable using Keepa and making it part of your essential toolkit

**Continue:**

* Keeping your eye on Account Health
* Being mindful of Late shipment notifications
* Handling and watching for Negative feedback
* A-to-Z claim filings
* Applying for ungates
* Leaning Keepa and learning how to source

## What kills new accounts

### 1. Ignoring customer messages

Amazon counts a customer message unanswered for 24+ hours as a defect. Five defects in 90 days flags your account.

**Fix:**

* Set up auto-response (Settings → Notifications)
* Check messages once daily, even on weekends
* Standard response: "Thanks for reaching out — I'll have a full answer within 24 hours."

### 2. Trying to bypass account holds

New accounts have funds held in reserve. Some sellers try to:

* Use multiple accounts
* Have family members open accounts
* Open business + personal accounts simultaneously

Amazon's anti-multi-account algorithm catches these. Result: instant suspension of all related accounts.

**Fix:** Be patient. Reserves release naturally as your account history builds.

### 3. Aggressive pricing

You discount to the bottom to win Buy Box, then competitors notice the listing's tanking and you're left holding inventory at a loss.

**Fix:**

* Don't undercut&#x20;
* Avoid "race to the bottom" pricing
* If you can't compete profitably, walk and pick a different SKU

## What helps new accounts

Five behaviors that build account strength fast:

### 1. Respond to every message within 4 hours

Faster than the 24-hour requirement. Amazon's algorithm rewards responsive sellers.

### 2. Prevent A-to-Z claims proactively

If a customer messages about a damaged unit, **immediately offer a refund or replacement** before they file. Filed A-to-Z claims hurt accounts more than refunds.

### 3. Maintain "Like New" condition rigor

Even on used books and grade-down items, be conservative. A "Used - Like New" listed honestly attracts fewer "Item not as described" complaints than aggressive grading.

### 4. Tag inventory carefully

Every unit at FBA should have:

* Correct FNSKU label
* Correct condition (don't list "used" as "new" ever)
* Correct quantity (don't ship one box and claim 50 units; ship 50 and claim 50)

### 5. Document everything

Keep:

* Receipts photographed
* Shipment tracking numbers
* Customer message threads
* Reimbursement case IDs
* Brand IP risk research notes

In a complaint dispute, evidence wins. Build the habit of documentation early.

## Monitoring Account Health daily

Your **Account Health Dashboard** (Seller Central → Performance → Account Health) shows:

| Metric                      | Target | Yellow zone | Red zone        |
| --------------------------- | ------ | ----------- | --------------- |
| Order Defect Rate (ODR)     | <1%    | 1–2%        | >2%             |
| Late Shipment Rate (LSR)    | <4%    | 4–7%        | >7% (FBM only)  |
| Pre-fulfillment Cancel Rate | <2.5%  | 2.5–3.5%    | >3.5%           |
| Valid Tracking Rate         | >95%   | 90–95%      | <90% (FBM only) |
| On-Time Delivery Rate       | >97%   | 95–97%      | <95% (FBM only) |

For FBA-only sellers, only ODR really matters. Watch it weekly.

If a metric trends yellow:

* Investigate the cause (which orders contributed?)
* Take corrective action (refund proactively, improve listings)
* Don't ignore — yellow → red → suspension if unaddressed

## What "graduating" looks like

After 90 days clean:

| Before                                         | After                                            |
| ---------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| Reserves: 14-day rolling                       | Reserves: usually 0 (some categories still hold) |
| Restricted ASINs: blocked from some            | Restricted ASINs: most accessible                |
| Appeals: harsh, lack of experience             | Appeals: easier to understand and handle         |
| IP claims: account-killer, not addressing them | IP claims: hurt, comfortable adressing them      |

You'll feel the shift around days 30–60. Trust the process, if you can get to 90 days you're set for success.

## Reset advice if things went wrong

If you've already had a suspension or major issue in your first 90 days:

1. **Don't quit.** Most sellers who push through come back stronger.
2. **Read the** [**suspension playbook**](../anti-patterns/suspension.md) for POA tactics.
3. **Pause sourcing** until the issue is fully resolved. Don't make it worse with more inventory at risk.
4. **Audit your sourcing process** — what decision led to the issue? What gets cut from your sourcing rules?
5. **Restart conservatively** — even after reinstatement, treat your account as still on probation for another 30 days.
