# Identity verification & video call

🟢 **Beginner.** Amazon's verification process tripped up tens of thousands of legitimate sellers in 2023–2025. Here's how to pass on the first try.

## What Amazon is verifying

Three things:
1. **You are a real person** — government ID, video call
2. **Your business is legitimate** — entity paperwork, EIN, address consistency
3. **You haven't been banned before** — name + address + payment method cross-check against ban list

The third one is the silent killer. Amazon links accounts via:
- Email address
- Phone number
- Bank account number
- Credit card
- Physical address
- Device fingerprint
- IP address

If any of these match a previously-banned account (yours or someone else's at your address), you'll be flagged.

## Document preparation

What works and what doesn't:

### Government ID

✅ **Drivers license** — front + back, both visible
✅ **Passport** — full-page photo of the photo page
✅ **State ID** — same as drivers license

❌ Expired ID
❌ Photo of ID on a screen (must be physical)
❌ Cropped images that cut off corners
❌ Heavily-shadowed or glared photos

**Best practice:** lay ID flat on a contrasting solid surface (white desk works), turn off ceiling lights, use phone's camera in good natural light. Take 4-corner-visible shot.

### Bank statement

✅ **Most recent month's statement** — full PDF from your bank
✅ Shows: bank name, your name (or LLC name) matching account, address matching application, account number (last 4 visible)
✅ Dated within 90 days

❌ Mobile banking app screenshot (Amazon usually rejects)
❌ Statement >90 days old
❌ Credit card statement when bank statement was requested

**Where to get it:** log into your bank's website (not the app), navigate to Statements, download PDF. Don't use a screenshot of your account dashboard.

### Utility bill

✅ Electric, gas, water, internet, or phone bill
✅ Dated within 90 days
✅ Address must match what's on your seller account

❌ Cell phone bill (Amazon sometimes rejects)
❌ Mortgage / rent statements (use a separate document if needed)

If you live with family or roommates and bills aren't in your name:
- Use a bank statement instead
- Or get the utility transferred to your name (takes 1–2 weeks)

## The "name match" trap

Amazon checks that **all three documents agree** on your name and address:

| Doc | Name | Address |
|---|---|---|
| Government ID | "John A. Smith" | "123 Main St, Apt 4, Boston, MA 02118" |
| Bank statement | "John Smith" | "123 Main St #4, Boston MA 02118" |
| Utility bill | "John A Smith" | "123 Main Street, Apt 4, Boston, MA" |

The variations above will sometimes pass and sometimes fail. **Make them consistent before submitting:**
- Update your bank profile to include middle initial if your ID has it
- Get utility bill that uses the exact same format as your ID
- If updating documents takes weeks, sometimes it's easier to update your driver's license format at the DMV

## The video call

This is where most beginners panic. The reality:

### What to expect

- A 5–15 minute video call with an Amazon verification rep
- Scheduled within 24–72 hours of submitting documents
- Usually conducted via a custom Amazon video link (not Zoom)
- Reps are based globally — you'll get someone from a contractor company

### What they ask

A typical script:
1. "Show me your government-issued ID, front and back."
2. "What's the legal name of your business?"
3. "What types of products do you plan to sell?"
4. "Where are you based?"
5. "Do you intend to source products yourself, or use a wholesaler?"
6. Sometimes: "Have you sold on Amazon before under any other account?"

### How to answer

✅ **Be brief.** "I plan to sell consumer products sourced from retail and online wholesalers."
✅ **Be honest.** Lying about sourcing or prior accounts triggers further investigation.
✅ **Make eye contact** with the camera, not the screen.

❌ **Don't over-explain** your business plan. They don't care; they're checking you exist.
❌ **Don't volunteer info** about prior accounts unless asked.
❌ **Don't take the call from a car or noisy place** — they may reschedule, delaying you a week.

### What if you're nervous

Most reps are not adversarial. They're checking boxes:
1. ID matches the person on screen
2. Person is coherent and answers basic questions
3. Person isn't reading from a script (which would suggest fraud)

A nervous, slightly stumbling answer is **better than a polished, scripted one.** Just be a real person.

### The "sourcing channels" question

Sometimes reps ask where you'll source. **Acceptable answers:**
- "Retail arbitrage and online arbitrage from major US retailers"
- "Wholesale from authorized distributors"
- "Mix of retail and wholesale"

**Don't say:**
- "Whatever I can find" (sounds sketchy)
- "From overseas suppliers I don't know yet" (counterfeiting flag)
- "I'm not sure yet" (can extend verification)

## Failure cases and recovery

### Fail: Document rejected

You'll get an email: "We couldn't verify your documents."
- **Re-submit higher-quality scans/photos**
- If rejected again, open a help case and ask which document failed
- Reps can sometimes guide you to specific issues

### Fail: Video call missed

Calendar invite came at a bad time, you missed it.
- Click the rescheduling link in the verification email
- Wait time: typically 3–7 days
- Don't miss the second slot — third misses risk full account closure

### Fail: Video call disconnected

Call started, internet dropped.
- Most reps will retry within 5 minutes
- If not, expect a rescheduling link within 24 hours

### Fail: Identity didn't match

You're a real person but Amazon's algo flagged you. Causes:
- Name spelling differences across documents
- Address inconsistencies
- Phone number changes recently
- Recent move (less than 30 days at current address)

Recovery: open a help case explaining the discrepancy with photo evidence (e.g., "I moved 45 days ago, here's my new lease and updated DL"). Resolution: 1–4 weeks.

### Fail: "Cannot verify your identity"

The dreaded final rejection. Causes:
- Algorithmic match against a previously-suspended account
- Heavy data-mismatch across documents
- Identity theft / fraud flag

Recovery options:
- Open multiple help cases asking for specific reasons
- Send written appeal to Amazon's address with notarized documents
- For high-stakes accounts, hire a reinstatement attorney

This happens to ~5% of legitimate sellers and is **brutal.** Most resolve eventually but the process can take 1–3 months.

## During the verification window

Your account is in "limited" mode until verified. Things you can/can't do:

| Activity | Limited mode |
|---|---|
| Browse Seller Central | ✅ Yes |
| Add ASINs to catalog | ✅ Yes |
| Create FBA shipments | ✅ Yes |
| Receive customer orders | ✅ Yes |
| Receive deposits from sales | ❌ No (held) |
| Apply for category ungating | 🟡 Some categories blocked |
| Use FBA for restricted brands | ❌ No |

Once verified, holds release and back-deposits flow within 14 days.

## Pro tips for first-timers

### Do verification on a desktop, not mobile
The Seller Central UI is more reliable on desktop. Mobile sometimes loses uploads.

### Don't use a VPN
Amazon's fraud detection flags VPN traffic. Even if you normally use one, disable it during verification.

### Use the same internet connection
Don't start verification at home, finish at coffee shop. IP address consistency matters.

### Don't pay for "verification services"
Scammers offer to "fix" your verification for \$200–\$1000. Amazon doesn't outsource verification, and these services are at best useless and at worst impersonating you (fraud).

### Have backup documents ready
If your first bank statement is rejected, you don't want to wait 30 days for the next one. Save 2–3 months of statements ahead of time.

## What gets accounts banned during verification

Behaviors that trigger immediate account closure:
- Lying on the application (especially about prior accounts)
- Using fake or photoshopped documents
- Multiple-account violation detected mid-verification
- Document evidence of identity theft (someone else's documents)
- Refusing the video call multiple times

Avoid all of these. The verification is annoying but legitimate sellers pass it.
