# Ungating playbooks (by retailer)

🟡 **Intermediate.** The canonical mechanic of ungating is unglamorous: buy 10 units of a brand from an authorized retailer → submit the invoice to Amazon → wait for approval → return the inventory. Net cost ≈ shipping + restock fees.

This page covers the exact recipes for the retailers that actually work. Most paid "ungating services" are doing exactly what's on this page, and charging \$200-\$500 markup for the convenience.

## The "buy 10, ungate, return" workflow

The de-facto standard ungating method:

### Step 1: Identify the brand or category you want ungated

Examples:
- **Brand** — Stanley, Yeti, Hot Wheels, Ninja, Cuisinart
- **Category** — Beauty, Grocery, Topicals, Health & Personal Care

### Step 2: Pick a retailer that sells the brand AND issues invoices

Critical filter — the retailer must:
- ✅ Issue invoices that include your **business name + address**
- ✅ Be considered "authorized" or "wholesale-ish" by Amazon
- ✅ Have an order-history portal you can download invoices from
- ✅ Accept returns within ≥ 30 days (so you can recover capital)

The retailers that meet all four are listed below.

### Step 3: Order 10 units of the brand-target item

10 is the empirically-known minimum that Amazon accepts. Some categories require fewer; some require more. **10 is a safe default.**

The unit doesn't have to be the exact ASIN you want to sell — it has to be the same **brand**. So 10 units of any cheap Stanley product ungates you for all Stanley.

### Step 4: Get the invoice

After delivery (NOT before — Amazon checks):
- Log into the retailer's order history
- Download the invoice as a PDF
- Verify it shows: brand name, quantity ≥ 10, your business name + address matching your seller-account profile

### Step 5: Submit to Amazon

Seller Central → Inventory → Add a Product → search for the gated ASIN → click "Apply for approval"

Upload the PDF invoice + any other documents requested.

### Step 6: Wait

Amazon's response: **24 hours to 14 days**. Most approvals come within 3-5 days.

### Step 7: Return the inventory

After approval:
- Return all 10 units to the retailer
- Receive refund (minus shipping in some cases)
- Net cost: shipping out + restocking fee (if any) ≈ \$15-\$60 per ungate

## Invoice-match checklist (non-negotiable)

The invoice MUST contain:

- ✅ **Quantity ≥ 10 units** of the brand-target item
- ✅ **Your business name** matching your seller-account profile exactly
- ✅ **Your address** matching your seller-account address exactly
- ✅ **Your email** matching your seller-account email
- ✅ **Your phone** matching your seller-account phone
- ✅ **Issued within 180 days** of the application
- ✅ **From an authorized retailer/distributor** (NOT eBay, Amazon, marketplace sellers)

Mismatches = automatic rejection. **Update your retailer profile to match your seller account profile before placing the order.**

## Per-retailer playbooks

The retailers with proven ungating success:

### B&H Photo (`bhphoto.com`)

**Best for:** ASUS, MSI, Fujifilm, Kodak, Canon, Epson, Nikon, electronics in general

**Account type:** Standard B&H account (no business account needed)

**Invoice path:**
1. Order History → click order
2. "Print Receipt" → save as PDF

**Tips:**
- B&H Photo invoices clearly list the brand, qty, and your billing address — clean format Amazon accepts
- Returns: 30 days, free return shipping label provided
- Tax-exempt status accepted with reseller cert

### Zoro (`zoro.com`)

**Best for:** Dewalt, Milwaukee, Brother, Schlage, Klein Tools, professional/industrial brands

**Account type:** **Business account REQUIRED** (sign up at `zoro.com/business`)

**Invoice path:**
1. Sign in → Order History
2. Click Order Number → "Download Invoice (PDF)"

**Tips:**
- Zoro is the cleanest source for tools, hardware, and industrial supplies
- Their invoices are explicitly formatted for B2B — Amazon loves them
- 30-day returns, full refund minus shipping

### Target (`target.com`)

**Best for:** LEGO (with documentation), Hot Wheels, Barbie, Ninja, Cuisinart, Neutrogena, Aveeno, Tylenol — toys/beauty/health

**Account type:** Standard Target account

**Invoice path:**
1. Account → Orders → click the order
2. "Receipts and Invoices" → "View Detailed Invoice"
3. Print to PDF

**Tips:**
- Target's invoices are simpler than wholesale invoices but Amazon accepts them for many brands
- Best for ungating mass-market consumer brands
- Returns: 90 days for most items
- LEGO ungating note: even with Target invoices, LEGO often requires additional documentation (LEGO authorized reseller paperwork). Don't expect Target alone to ungate LEGO.

### iHerb (`iherb.com`)

**Best for:** Aveeno, Neutrogena, Nature's Bounty, Vital Proteins, Optimum Nutrition, supplements, vitamins, premium beauty

**Account type:** Standard

**Invoice path:**
1. My Account → Order History
2. Click order → "View Invoice"
3. Save as PDF

**Tips:**
- iHerb is the supplements/vitamins gold standard
- Their invoices include detailed product info and meet Amazon's documentation standards
- Returns: 60 days
- International shipping available — useful if domestic supply is constrained

### Best Buy (`bestbuy.com`)

**Best for:** LEGO, Razer, Pokemon, Brother, Nintendo, Canon, vinyl/DVDs, grocery, electronics

**Account type:** **Business account REQUIRED** for orders > 1 unit. Sign up at `bestbuy.com/business`

**Invoice path:**
1. Account → Order History
2. Click order → "Print Invoice"

**Critical caveat:**
- ⚠️ **Shipped delivery only** — in-store pickup invoices DO NOT WORK for Amazon ungating
- If you're tempted to use BOPIS to save shipping, don't — the receipt format differs

**Tips:**
- Best Buy's business account opens up bulk-buying capacity
- Returns: 14-30 days depending on item type
- Electronics returns are stricter — open-box may incur restocking fee

### Vitacost (`vitacost.com`)

**Best for:** Vitamins, supplements, organic foods, beauty (overlaps iHerb)

**Account type:** Standard

**Invoice path:**
1. My Account → Order History → "View/Print Invoice"

**Tips:**
- Alternative to iHerb when iHerb doesn't carry the brand
- Returns: 30 days
- Tax-exempt available

### Costco Business (`costcobusiness.com`)

**Best for:** Bulk grocery, household, some health items, generic brands

**Account type:** Costco Business membership required (\$60/year)

**Invoice path:**
1. Sign in → Order Status → "View Invoice"

**Tips:**
- Bulk pricing means lower per-unit cost for ungating runs
- Returns: 30-90 days depending on category
- Membership cost amortizes over multiple ungating runs

### Sam's Club Business (`samsclub.com/business`)

**Best for:** Bulk grocery, household, beauty closeouts, electronics

**Account type:** Sam's Club Business membership

**Invoice path:**
1. Account → Purchase History → "View Receipt"

**Tips:**
- Similar to Costco Business — bulk-friendly
- Their "Plus" tier includes bulk-shipping at lower per-unit rates

### Walmart Business (`business.walmart.com`)

**Best for:** Mass-market consumer goods across categories

**Account type:** Walmart Business account (free to sign up)

**Invoice path:**
1. Account → Orders → "Print Invoice"

**Tips:**

- Free to sign up; no minimum orders

### Office Depot Business (`business.officedepot.com`)

**Best for:** Office supplies, cleaning, breakroom snacks, ink/toner, electronics

**Account type:** Office Depot Business

**Invoice path:**

### Staples Business (`stapless.com`)

**Best for:** Office supplies, ink/toner, electronics, small appliances

## What doesn't work

Retailers that look promising but consistently fail:

- ❌ **Amazon itself** — Amazon won't accept their own invoices for ungating
- ❌ **eBay** — marketplace, not authorized
- ❌ **Walmart consumer (non-business)** — receipts often rejected
- ❌ **Costco consumer** — same issue
- ❌ **TJ Maxx, Ross, Marshalls** — discount retailers, no authorization
- ❌ **Liquidation sites** (Liquidation.com, B-Stock, BULQ) — explicitly unauthorized

## Common rejection reasons + fixes

### Rejection: "Documents do not match seller account"
- Invoice address differs from seller account by even one character
- **Fix:** update seller profile or retailer profile to match exactly; reapply after order

### Rejection: "Insufficient documentation"
- Quantity < 10 units
- **Fix:** order 10+ units next time; Amazon's threshold is consistent

### Rejection: "Source not authorized"
- The retailer isn't on Amazon's "authorized" list for that brand
- **Fix:** try a different retailer from the playbook above

### Rejection: "Need additional brand authorization"
- The brand requires a Letter of Authorization (LOA) from the brand owner
- **Fix:** Tier 1 brands (Disney, Nintendo, etc.) may require LOA — these often have NO retail-invoice path. Walk from the brand if you can't get LOA.

### Rejection: "Application denied without specific reason"
- Generic denial; usually means category-level restriction
- **Fix:** wait 90 days, build account history, reapply

## When to pay an ungating service

Some BoxedUp members pay services for ungating. Worth it if:
- ✅ You don't want to handle the buy-return logistics
- ✅ The service has connections for hard-to-ungate brands (LOA paths)
- ✅ Your time is worth more than the markup

NOT worth it if:
- ❌ The brand is on the easy-retailer list above
- ❌ You can do the buy-10-return loop in 1-2 hours of work

Most easy ungatings cost \$200-\$500 from a service. If your time is worth less than that for 2 hours of work, do it yourself.

## After ungating: maintenance

Once you're ungated for a brand:

### Don't lose it
- Don't get inauthentic complaints (kills the ungating retroactively)
- Save your original ungating invoice forever (you may need to re-prove)
- Source from authorized channels going forward

### Scale into it
- Now that you're ungated, build replens within the brand
- Source from cheaper channels (RA, OA) once Amazon trusts you
- Eventually graduate to wholesale relationships for that brand

## The big picture

Ungating is a **one-time tax** to access a brand or category. Pay it once, source forever (within reason).

Treat it as infrastructure investment, not a recurring cost. Budget \$50-\$150 per ungating attempt; expect 70-80% success rate from clean retailer playbooks.

Stack ungatings strategically: every 2-3 months, ungate one new brand or category that complements your existing inventory.
