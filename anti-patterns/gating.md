# Gated brands, gated categories

🟡 **Intermediate.** Amazon "gates" certain brands and categories — you can't sell in them without applying for approval first. Hitting a gated listing for the first time is a beginner rite of passage. Knowing the rules saves you from sourcing inventory you can't sell.

## The two types of gating

### Brand gating
Specific brands require permission to sell. The brand owner has registered with Amazon Brand Registry and Amazon enforces it.

Examples (not exhaustive):
- **Always gated**: Apple, Disney, Nintendo, LEGO (mostly), Sony, Bose, Beats, Adidas, Nike, Under Armour, Nestlé, P&G premium lines
- **Often gated**: Stanley, Hydro Flask, Yeti, KitchenAid, Dyson
- **Sometimes gated**: depends on category + your account history

### Category gating
Whole categories require approval, regardless of brand. Examples:
- **Health & Personal Care** — health risk
- **Beauty** — counterfeit risk
- **Topicals** (creams, ointments) — health/IP risk
- **Grocery & Gourmet Food** — perishable, food safety
- **Watches** — counterfeit-prone
- **Jewelry** — high-counterfeit
- **Automotive** (some sub-categories) — safety
- **Toys** during Q4 — sometimes Q4-only restrictions

## How to check before sourcing

Two ways to verify gating status before you spend money:

### Method 1: SellerAmp / BoxedUp scanner
Modern scanners check gating in real-time. When you scan a UPC/ASIN, you'll see one of:
- ✅ **Eligible** — you're already approved
- 🟡 **Approval required** — you can apply but haven't yet
- 🔴 **Restricted** — Amazon won't let you sell, period

This is the **fastest gating check.** Always scan before buying.

### Method 2: Amazon "Sell on Amazon" button
In Seller Central → Catalog → Add a product → search for the ASIN. Click "Sell yours."

You'll see:
- A "Sell" button → you're eligible
- "Apply for approval" → you can request access
- "Cannot list this product" → restricted, no path

This works without scanning hardware but is slower.

## The "buy 10, ungate, return" workflow

Before getting into application paths — there's a canonical method most resellers use that bypasses traditional wholesale paperwork:

1. **Buy 10 units** of the target brand from an authorized retailer
2. **Submit the retail invoice** to Amazon as ungating documentation
3. **Get approved**
4. **Return the inventory** to the retailer for refund

Net cost: ~\$15-\$60 per ungate (shipping + restocking fees if any).

This is the de-facto standard for ungating common brands and categories. Detailed per-retailer recipes (B&H Photo, Zoro, Target, iHerb, Best Buy, Walmart Business, etc.): **[Ungating playbooks (by retailer) →](ungating-playbooks.md)**.

Most paid "ungating services" are doing exactly this loop and charging \$200-\$500 markup for the convenience.

## Applying for ungating

When a listing says "Apply for approval," you can submit an application. The process varies:

### Light-touch ungating (no documentation)
Some brands/categories will auto-approve based on your account history. Click "Apply" → instant approval. Lucky.

### Invoice-required ungating
Most gates require an invoice with these specs:

**Invoice-match checklist (non-negotiable):**
- ✅ **Quantity ≥ 10 units** of the brand-target item
- ✅ **Your business name** matching your seller-account profile EXACTLY
- ✅ **Your address** matching your seller-account address EXACTLY
- ✅ **Your email** matching your seller-account email
- ✅ **Your phone** matching your seller-account phone
- ✅ **Issued within 180 days** of the application
- ✅ **From an authorized retailer or distributor** (NOT eBay, Amazon, marketplace sellers)

Even one-character mismatches trigger automatic rejection. Update your retailer profile to match seller-account profile before placing the order.

**For mainstream consumer brands**, the buy-10-ungate-return loop using authorized retailers (Target, B&H Photo, Zoro, iHerb, Best Buy Business, Walmart Business) works most of the time. See [ungating playbooks](ungating-playbooks.md).

**For Tier-1 IP-aggressive brands** (Disney, Nintendo, premium luxury), retail invoices won't suffice. You need a **Letter of Authorization (LOA)** from the brand directly — usually impossible without an established wholesale relationship. Walk from these brands until established.

⚠️ **Walmart consumer receipts almost never work** for big-name brand ungating. You need Walmart **Business** account invoices, not regular receipts. Same logic applies to Costco — the **Costco Business** invoices work; consumer ones don't.

To get a real wholesale invoice (the alternative path):
- Sign up as a wholesale customer with a brand or distributor
- Place a small order (10+ units of the cheapest item)
- Use the invoice for Amazon ungating

### Document-required ungating
Some gates require:
- LLC paperwork
- EIN
- Reseller permit
- Letter of authorization from the brand
- Proof of authenticity (photos, certificates)

The "letter of authorization" is the kicker — many brands won't issue these to small resellers. If a category requires LOA, treat it as effectively closed unless you have brand contacts.

## What gets you ungated and what doesn't

### Categories that ungate easily (with effort)
- **Grocery / Gourmet Food** — invoice from a wholesale distributor, ~70% approval
- **Health & Personal Care** — sometimes auto-approved, sometimes invoice
- **Beauty** — invoice from a real distributor
- **Toys & Games** (Q4 lockdown excluded) — usually open

### Brands that are realistically ungatable
- Most CPG (Pepsi, P&G, Unilever) — wholesale invoice works
- Many mid-tier toy brands — invoice works
- Most non-major beauty (think Korean beauty, indie skincare) — invoice + persistence

### Brands you basically can't ungate
- Apple, Disney, Nintendo, LEGO (without LEGO authorization)
- Pokémon, Marvel, Star Wars
- Most luxury (Chanel, Gucci, LV)
- Most pharmaceuticals
- Anything with "Authorized Dealer" requirements

## The Q4 toy gate

Every year around October, Amazon adds Q4 restrictions to certain categories — most famously **Toys**. Sellers below certain thresholds (varies year to year) get locked out of new toy listings until January.

**Approximate thresholds (vary):**
- 25+ orders shipped from Sep 1 onward
- ODR < 1%
- Late shipment rate < 4%
- Cancellation rate < 1.75%

If you're below the threshold, Q4 toys are locked — no new toy ASINs, sometimes not even new units of existing ASINs.

**Plan ahead:** if you intend to play Q4 toys, ramp orders in August/September to clear the threshold. Don't wait until November to find out you're locked out.

## What "restricted" actually means

When a listing says "Cannot list this product":

- It's beyond brand gating — Amazon has decided you can't sell it
- Reasons: counterfeit risk too high, brand-level enforcement, account-specific restriction
- Application path is closed
- Sourcing this listing wastes money — **you cannot list it, period**

Always trust the "restricted" status. Don't try to work around it (changing SKU, etc.) — that's how accounts get suspended.

## What to do if you sourced gated/restricted

You bought 50 units, then realized it's gated. Options:

1. **Apply for ungating** — try the application flow first; you might get lucky
2. **Sell elsewhere** — eBay, Mercari, Facebook Marketplace, Walmart Marketplace
3. **Return to retailer** — within return window with receipt
4. **Wholesale to another reseller** — Discord buy/sell channels, Reddit r/Amzreseller
5. **Hold until brand opens** — sometimes brands ungate themselves later
6. **Eat the loss** — last resort

The more often this happens, the more you should scan first, source second.
