# Roles, tiers, and how to level up

🟢 **Beginner.** Discord roles control what you can see and do. Higher tiers get access to better deals and earlier signals. This page explains how the BoxedUp role system typically works.

> **Note:** The exact role names and access levels in your server may differ. Check `#welcome` for the canonical list.

## Free vs paid tiers

Most BoxedUp-style servers are **paid community access** — small monthly fee that funds the bots, monitors, and curation work. Common tier structure:

### 🟢 Member (free or trial)
What you get:
- Access to `#general`, `#wins-and-losses`, learning channels
- Read-only on most deal feeds
- Public Q&A threads
- This GitBook guide

What you don't get:
- Live A2A pings
- Restock monitor alerts
- Tempo/Blaze setup
- Wholesale leads

### 🟡 Sourcer (mid-tier paid)
What you get:
- Everything in Member, plus:
- Live `#a2a-feed` pings
- `#restock-monitors` alerts
- `#kpf-queries` access
- Walmart/Target clearance feeds

This is the typical entry tier for active resellers.

### 🔴 Pro Sourcer (top-tier paid)
What you get:
- Everything in Sourcer, plus:
- `#wholesale-leads` (curated, vetted opportunities)
- Tempo/Blaze monitor setup support
- Direct mod/founder access for sourcing strategy questions
- Priority response on `#account-issues`

For sellers running >\$10K/mo, this tier pays for itself in a single quarter.

### 🟠 Whale / Founding Member (closed)
- Often a closed tier — invite-only or grandfathered
- Access to hot-mic conversations between top sourcers
- First-look on new tooling

If you don't have access, you don't need to worry about it. Whales aren't holding out on you — they're just the longest-tenured members.

## How to advance tiers

Three paths:

### Path 1: Subscribe up
Most direct. Hit the upgrade flow in the server's Patreon / Whop / paid-membership integration.

### Path 2: Earn it through contribution
Some servers have an "earned" tier path:
- Surface your own valuable leads in deal channels
- Write quality content for `#case-studies`
- Help newer members in `#sourcing-help`
- Maintain a positive reputation (good reactions, no drama)

After ~3–6 months of high-signal contribution, mods may invite you to a paid tier at a discount or for free.

### Path 3: Referrals + community building
Some servers offer tier upgrades for referring new paying members. Read the server's specific terms before promising friends an upgrade.

## Server-specific roles

Beyond access tiers, you'll see specialty roles:

### 🎓 Veteran
Members who've been around 12+ months and have demonstrated expertise. Soft mod role — they help new members. Often the first responders in `#sourcing-help`.

### 🛠️ Mod
Active moderators. Don't ping them for casual questions — only when:
- Reporting a rule violation
- Asking about server logistics
- Onboarding issues

### 📦 Wholesale Vet
Members with established wholesale supplier relationships. They're often the ones posting in `#wholesale-leads`. Expect them to be guarded — wholesale relationships are competitive moats.

### 🎯 Tempo / Blaze Helper
Members who help newer folks set up their monitor commands. Tag them in `#bot-commands` if you're stuck.

### 👑 Founder / Owner
The person who runs the server. Don't pollute their DMs with general questions — mods handle those. DM the founder only for partnership/business questions.

## Reaction culture

The Discord uses reactions as a soft-currency:

| Reaction | Meaning |
|---|---|
| ✅ | "I acted on this" |
| 💰 | "I bought this — thanks" |
| 🔥 | "This is exceptional" |
| 👀 | "Watching / monitoring" |
| 👏 | "Great write-up" |
| 🙏 | "Thanks for the help" |
| 🤔 | "Hmm, interesting / questioning" |

When someone surfaces a deal that converts for you, react with ✅ or 💰. It tells them their leads work and motivates more posts. **Reaction-cheap = acknowledgment-rich.**

## DM etiquette

Most members are open to DMs for:
- Specific sourcing questions you don't want public
- "Hey, I'd love to learn more about how you handle X"
- Wholesale-relationship intros (with offering value, not just asking)

Members are NOT open to DMs for:
- "Sell me your wholesale relationships"
- Cold pitches for services
- Asking them to do your sourcing for you
- "Quick question" that's actually 20 minutes of help

When in doubt: post in `#sourcing-help` first. If a member responds and the conversation deepens, then DM is fine.

## What gets you removed

Behaviors that lose access:

❌ **Sharing paid content elsewhere** — screenshots of deals to non-members, paid-group sharing
❌ **Reselling membership access** — letting a friend log in on your account
❌ **Cold DM spam** — pitching services unsolicited to many members
❌ **Drama** — public arguments, harassment, doxing
❌ **Crypto/MLM pitches** — instant ban
❌ **Fake wins** — posting fabricated screenshots of profit

The bar isn't high — be a real person, contribute, don't be weird. 99% of members have no issues.

## Reactivating after a lapse

If you let a paid tier expire and want back in:
- DM mods or use the `#account-issues` channel
- Most servers will re-onboard you without friction
- Some servers re-charge a setup fee for repeat lapses (rare)
