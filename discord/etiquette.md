# Discord etiquette (don't be that guy)

🟢 **Beginner.** The unwritten social rules that determine whether you become a respected member or get muted/banned. Most are obvious; some aren't.

## The Discord social contract

The community works when members:
1. Contribute more than they extract
2. Respect channel purposes
3. Don't waste others' time
4. Treat veterans + newer members with patience
5. Skip the drama

Violate enough of these and you'll find yourself tagged out.

## The 12 rules

### Rule 1: Read before posting

Before asking a question:
- Search Discord (Ctrl+F)
- Read the GitBook (the page you're on)
- Check pinned messages in the channel
- Check `#faq` if it exists

99% of beginner questions have been asked. Asking a question that's been answered 50 times before clutters everyone's notifications.

### Rule 2: Use the right channel

Specific channels exist for specific purposes:
- Don't post deals in `#general`
- Don't post account issues in `#wins-and-losses`
- Don't post questions in `#announcements` (read-only)

If you're not sure which channel: read pinned messages in `#welcome` for a channel map. Or check the [channel map](channels.md).

### Rule 3: React, don't type "+1"

A 👍 reaction = visible to everyone, doesn't notify.
"+1" reply = notification to the original poster, and clutters the channel.

Reactions also count as social signal:
- `✅` "I acted on this"
- `💰` "Bought this — thanks"
- `🔥` "Exceptional content"
- `🙏` "Thanks for sharing"

### Rule 4: Drop ASIN, then numbers, then question

A useful sourcing-help post:
```
ASIN: B0XXXXX
Cost $8 (Walmart in-store, receipt attached)
Buy Box $25, BSR 8K (top 1%), 2 FBA sellers
[Keepa link]
ROI looks great but worried about offer count spike May → present.
Anyone else seeing this trend or am I overthinking?
```

A useless post:
```
@everyone is this a good deal??
[blurry photo of a Walmart shelf]
```

The first one is answerable in 30 seconds. The second one is a request for free consulting.

### Rule 5: Don't ping `@everyone` or `@here`

These pings notify EVERY member with notifications enabled. Reserved for:
- Genuine emergencies
- Server-wide announcements (mods only)

Use sparingly. Members will mute you (or worse) if you abuse it.

### Rule 6: Don't ping veterans for everything

Veterans + mods have lives. Pinging "@VetName" with every question gets you ignored fast.

Better:
- Post in the appropriate channel (`#sourcing-help`)
- Wait for whoever's online to respond
- DM only after exhausting public channels AND only with high-effort questions

### Rule 7: Don't repost paid-group leads

If you're in another paid Discord/Telegram group, don't bring their leads here. It's:
- Theft of their content
- Bannable in many BoxedUp servers
- Gets BoxedUp blacklisted from cross-server cooperation

Internal leads only. Or post your own original sourcing finds.

### Rule 8: Never DM-pitch services

Cold-DMing 100 members about your "Amazon coaching" or "prep service" or "wholesale list" gets you banned within hours.

If you offer a service:
- Make a single post in `#self-promotion` (if it exists)
- DM mods first to ask if pitching is allowed
- Otherwise, just be a member; let people DM you organically

### Rule 9: Don't spam your wins

Sharing a big win occasionally in `#wins-and-losses` is welcome. Sharing every \$50 sale becomes spam.

Rule of thumb: 1–2 win posts per week max for established members. New members: even less, until you've contributed value first.

### Rule 10: Don't argue publicly

Disagreement happens. Handle it:
- ✅ DM the person to discuss privately
- ✅ Post a follow-up with new perspective in the same thread
- ❌ Public argument that drags 10 messages
- ❌ Personal attacks

Mods will close threads or remove participants who escalate.

### Rule 11: Don't leak server content

Some BoxedUp servers have rules against:
- Screenshotting deals to outside groups
- Recording mod calls
- Sharing wholesale lead info to non-members

Treat the server like a paid newsletter. Subscribers see the content; non-subscribers don't.

### Rule 12: When you make a mistake, own it

If you violated a rule:
- Acknowledge the mistake publicly
- Don't argue with the mod who flagged it
- Move on

Members who handle mod actions gracefully recover quickly. Members who fight every mod action get permanent bans.

## Notifications discipline

Configure Discord notifications by channel:

### Server-wide settings
- 🔕 Mute the server's `@everyone` notifications
- Allow specific channel pushes only

### Per-channel settings
- 🔔 **All messages**: deal-feed channels during active hours
- 🔔 **Mentions only**: most channels
- 🔕 **Muted**: status channels, low-signal channels

### Mobile vs desktop
- Mobile: critical channels only (deal pings)
- Desktop: more channels (you're at the computer anyway)

If your Discord constantly buzzes, you'll burn out and miss real signals. Tighten notifications proactively.

## Engaging with new members

When you see someone new:

✅ **Say hi in `#introductions`** — small effort, big positive impact
✅ **Answer their first question patiently** — even if it's been asked before
✅ **Recommend the GitBook** — saves you from explaining basics
✅ **Compliment their first wins** — encourage progress

❌ **Don't condescend** — "everyone here knows that" attitude pushes new members out
❌ **Don't joke at their expense** — what's funny to veterans isn't to beginners
❌ **Don't make them earn respect via hazing** — that's bad culture

The community gets stronger when veterans uplift newer members.

## Reaction culture

Reactions are the social currency:

- **`✅`** — confirms / agrees / acted-on
- **`💰`** — bought this; profit secured
- **`🔥`** — exceptional content (use sparingly)
- **`👀`** — watching / monitoring; will revisit
- **`🤔`** — interesting / questioning; not sure yet
- **`🙏`** — thanks for the help
- **`👏`** — great write-up / great win

Three uses:
1. **Acknowledge without notification** — quicker than typing
2. **Signal to the OP that their content is valued** — encouragement to post more
3. **Help mods see what's working** — high-reaction posts get pinned/promoted

## DM etiquette

When DM'ing another member:

✅ **Have a specific question** — "Quick question about your wholesale process: do you require Net-30 typically or Net-15?"
✅ **Respect their time** — be brief, direct
✅ **Offer value** — share what you know, ask what you don't
✅ **Thank them** — most members respond out of generosity

❌ **Don't say "u up?" or just "hi"** — wasted message
❌ **Don't ask for "10 minutes" of free coaching** — they're not a coach
❌ **Don't push for wholesale relationships** unless they invited it
❌ **Don't ghost after asking** — close the loop

## Voice channel etiquette

Some servers have voice rooms:

- Mute by default; only unmute when speaking
- Don't dominate (talking >50% in a 5-person call = bad)
- Echo cancellation: use a headset, not laptop speakers
- Don't broadcast without permission (sourcing in store with VC live = annoys others)

## Self-promotion handling

Most BoxedUp servers prohibit self-promotion outside specific channels:

✅ **Allowed:**
- Sharing your own success stories in `#wins-and-losses`
- Helping members in your areas of expertise
- Building organic relationships

❌ **Not allowed:**
- Promoting your YouTube channel for views
- Selling courses or coaching
- Recruiting for your prep service unsolicited
- Crypto / MLM / unrelated business pitches

If unsure: ask mods first.

## Thread etiquette

Long discussions belong in threads:

- Right-click message → Create Thread
- Continue conversation there
- Notifies only thread participants

When NOT to use a thread:
- Quick 2-message exchanges (just reply in main)
- Public-interest questions (let everyone see)
- Low-value chatter

When TO use a thread:
- Sourcing-help question with 10+ replies
- Deep-dive discussion on a topic
- Sub-conversation on a popular announcement

## Lurker culture

It's okay to lurk! Most members lurk before contributing:

- Read for a week+ before posting
- Get a feel for tone, channel norms, key people
- React to messages to start showing your engagement
- Post your introduction when comfortable

Long-time lurkers who finally post often surprise the community with depth. No rush.

## When to leave the server

Sometimes Discord stops fitting:

- You've outgrown the level of discussion
- Drama / culture has soured
- Your business pivoted away from arbitrage
- You need fewer notifications in your life

Leaving is fine. Don't burn bridges:
- Don't post a "leaving forever" rant
- Just leave quietly
- Re-join later if interests align

## When to recommend the server to others

Be selective. Recommend BoxedUp Discord if:

- They're serious about Amazon reselling
- They'll contribute, not just extract
- They can afford the membership (if paid)
- You'd vouch for them

Avoid:
- Recommending for "anyone who wants to make money online"
- Inviting friends without explaining the culture first
- Bringing in members who'll dilute the signal

## Final mantra

Three things to remember:

1. **Be useful** — share more than you ask
2. **Be patient** — others were beginners once; you'll be again with new tools
3. **Be respectful** — these are real people with their own lives

The Discord is a community. Treat it like one.
