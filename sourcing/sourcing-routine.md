# Building a sourcing routine that scales

🟡 → 🔴 — Once you've graduated from "scan everything that moves" to "I know my game", you need a routine. This page lays out a reproducible weekly cadence that scales from \$2K/month to \$50K+/month without proportional time investment.

## The wrong default

Beginners' default routine: **"sourcing happens when I have time."**

This breaks down because:
- Big wins require being there at the right moment (deal pings, restocks, KPF queries)
- "When I have time" = inconsistent, easy to skip, no compounding
- Scaling requires systematic reps, not opportunistic ones

The fix: a calendar block schedule + named routines per timeslot.

## The ideal weekly cadence

Times below are guidelines — adjust to your schedule. The structure is what matters.

### Monday morning (90 min)
**Purpose:** Capture restock signals from the weekend; set monitor list; plan the week.

| Block | Activity |
|---|---|
| 0:00–0:15 | Check overnight Tempo/Blaze pings; act on hot ones |
| 0:15–0:45 | Run KPF Restock query for primary category; triage |
| 0:45–1:15 | Add new high-conviction monitors via Tempo command |
| 1:15–1:30 | Review last week's wins / losses; update Found Profit List |

### Monday evening (60 min)
**Purpose:** Process inventory received over the weekend.

| Block | Activity |
|---|---|
| 0:00–0:30 | Build shipment for any inventory awaiting prep |
| 0:30–0:60 | Print labels, tape boxes, stage for carrier pickup |

### Tuesday–Thursday daytime (passive)
**Purpose:** Stream Keepa Deals + monitor pings while doing other work.

- Keepa Deals tab open in browser (A2A surface)
- Discord notifications for `#a2a-feed`, `#restock-monitors`
- Act on profitable pings within 5 minutes
- Aim for 1–5 buys per active hour

### Tuesday/Thursday evening (60 min)
**Purpose:** OA sourcing or store runs (alternating).

- Tuesday: Online arbitrage — work through your saved Keepa searches and deal channels
- Thursday: Retail arbitrage — visit 2–3 stores, scan clearance/end caps

### Friday morning (60 min)
**Purpose:** Operations + accounting.

| Block | Activity |
|---|---|
| 0:00–0:30 | Reimbursement claim review (SellerBoard / Refunds Manager) |
| 0:30–0:45 | Pricing audit — check your top 20 SKUs for tank risk |
| 0:45–1:00 | Update bookkeeping; reconcile bank vs Amazon deposit |

### Saturday or Sunday (30 min)
**Purpose:** Reflection + planning.

- Review last week's profit (SellerBoard P&L)
- Identify what worked / didn't
- Update next week's KPF query priorities
- Prune dead monitors from Tempo/Blaze

## Time budget — total per week

The above schedule:
- Monday: 2.5 hours
- Tue–Thu daytime: passive
- Tue/Thu evening: 2 hours
- Friday: 1 hour
- Weekend: 0.5 hours

**Total active time: ~6 hours/week.** This scales to \$10K–\$30K/month for most members. Past that, you're hiring help.

## The Found Profit List

A spreadsheet (or BoxedUp CC's Leads tab) of every profitable item you've ever found. Each row:

| ASIN | Source | Cost | Sell | ROI | Velocity | First found | Last bought | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| B0XXX | Walmart in-store | \$8 | \$25 | 80% | 50/mo | 2025-01-15 | 2025-04-20 | Active replen |
| B0YYY | Keepa Deals | \$32 | \$48 | 30% | 5/mo | 2025-02-08 | 2025-02-08 | One-time A2A |
| B0ZZZ | KPF Restock | \$45 | \$120 | 80% | 20/mo | 2025-03-12 | 2025-04-12 | Monitor live |

Review weekly. Patterns will emerge:
- Which sources produce most profit
- Which categories you actually win in
- Which "losers" should drop off your monitor list
- Which replens are worth reordering

The Found Profit List is the **compounding asset of arbitrage.** New members source ad-hoc and forget; veterans build a list that gets smarter every month.

## Replens: the scale unlock

Replens (replenishable products you can re-source from the same channel) are how arbitrage scales. The math:

- **Pure RA (no replens)**: 200 hours sourcing → 200 hours profit ceiling (linear)
- **RA + replens**: 200 hours initial sourcing → indefinite profit ceiling (replens compound)

Build replens by:

### 1. Tag every flip in your Found Profit List
"One-time" vs "Replenishable" — most flips fall into one. Replens are the keepers.

### 2. Reorder cadence
Set a calendar reminder for each replen:
- Weekly check-in for hot replens (sell-through < 7 days)
- Monthly check-in for steady replens
- Quarterly check-in for slow replens

### 3. Inventory levels
Hold inventory equal to ~30 days of velocity at FBA. Past that, storage fees + IPI degradation eat into margins.

### 4. Replen pruning
A replen stops being a replen when:
- Source stops carrying it
- Amazon orange line returns (Amazon back in stock = you lose Buy Box)
- Offer count saturates (>15 FBA sellers)
- ROI drops below 20%

Drop them from your active replen list and move on.

## The "deep work" block

In the schedule above, Tuesday/Thursday evenings (2 hours each) are deep-work blocks. During these:

- ❌ Phone in another room
- ❌ Discord muted (except your top-priority channels)
- ❌ No store-running with friends ("We'll just stop at one place" → 4 hours gone)
- ✅ Single-tasking — one of: KPF, store run, OA scan, Tempo monitor build
- ✅ Time-boxed — 2 hours, then stop

Deep work compounds. Two 2-hour focus blocks beat 8 hours of distracted scanning.

## Scaling beyond ~\$30K/month

When the 6-hour/week routine maxes out:

### Step 1: Outsource prep
- Prep service (\$0.50–\$1.50/unit + minimum)
- Frees up 2–4 hours/week of prep time
- See [Prep requirements](../fba/prep-requirements.md) for prep-service details

### Step 2: Outsource sourcing
- Hire a VA to run KPF queries + initial scoring
- You review their output (1 hour) instead of building queries (3 hours)
- Costs: \$5–\$15/hour for offshore VAs

### Step 3: Outsource shipping
- Most prep services include shipment-building
- Or use a 3PL (third-party logistics) for pure outsourcing

### Step 4: Outsource bookkeeping
- E-commerce CPA + monthly bookkeeper
- Your time goes to strategy, not data entry

By Step 4, you're at \$50K–\$200K/month and your role is "sourcing director" + business owner, not "person who scans things."

## When to add a partner

Some BoxedUp members partner up. Math:

| Solo at \$10K/mo | Partner at \$20K/mo |
|---|---|
| 100% of profit (~\$3K) | 50% of profit (~\$3K) |
| All operations (12 hrs/week) | Half operations (6 hrs/week) |

Partner math works only if:
- Their skillset complements yours (you source, they handle ops)
- Trust is rock-solid
- Profit-share + role is documented (LLC operating agreement)
- Both contribute equally (no "10% partner who does nothing")

Most BoxedUp members do better solo + outsourcing than with partners. Partners are emotionally costly when they don't carry their weight.

## What good looks like

A BoxedUp member running this routine for 12+ months:

- 50–100 active replens
- Found Profit List of 500+ flips with patterns identified
- 6 hours/week active sourcing
- ~\$10K–\$30K monthly revenue
- 25–35% net margins
- IPI > 700
- 0 active suspensions, 0 pending IP claims

Past \$30K/month with the same routine = scaling problem. Either outsource (Step 1–4 above) or accept a different lifestyle.

## What scaling failure looks like

Counter-example — patterns that prevent compounding:

- **Sourcing only when bored** — random, no Found Profit List, no replens
- **Chasing every deal-feed ping** — burns time on low-quality leads
- **No KPF / no monitors** — entirely reactive, no systematic discovery
- **Prepping every unit personally past \$10K/mo** — your time is now too valuable
- **No bookkeeping discipline** — surprise tax bills, unclear margins
- **Reinventing the wheel each week** — no routine = no compounding

Fix any one of these and your hourly rate doubles. Fix all five and you 10× your business.
