# Wholesale

🔴 **Advanced.** Wholesale = buying directly from a brand or distributor at trade pricing, then reselling on Amazon. Lower margins per unit than RA/OA, but **infinite scale** — same SKU 1000 units this month, 1000 next month, forever.

## Why wholesale matters

The math:

| Channel | Typical ROI | Typical margin | Volume scale |
|---|---|---|---|
| RA | 50–100% | 30–40% | Limited (1 store, 5 units) |
| OA | 30–60% | 20–30% | Medium (1 retailer, 50 units) |
| Wholesale | 15–30% | 12–20% | Infinite (1000+ units) |

Wholesale's lower per-unit margin is offset by 10–100× the volume. Once you have 5–10 wholesale relationships running, you're shipping containers, not boxes.

## The wholesale path

### Step 1: Find the brand
- Brand has an Amazon listing you've sourced before via RA/OA
- The listing has stable Buy Box, decent BSR, reasonable competition
- Brand isn't massive (Coke, P&G) — those are vendor-direct only

### Step 2: Find their wholesale program
- Search "[brand name] wholesale" or "[brand name] become a reseller"
- Many brands have a "wholesale@" or "trade@" email
- LinkedIn the sales/distribution team
- Trade show contacts (NY Now, Inspired Home, ASD Las Vegas)

### Step 3: Apply

Most brands require:
- **Business entity** — LLC paperwork
- **EIN**
- **Reseller certificate** — for tax-exempt purchases
- **Bank verification** — sometimes
- **References** — sometimes ask for 2 prior wholesale relationships
- **Website** — many require a "professional online presence"

For new resellers without prior wholesale experience, the website is the rough one. Solution: build a simple Shopify or website showing you're a real business. Doesn't need to sell anything — just look professional.

### Step 4: Negotiate terms

Once approved, you'll discuss:
- **MSRP / MAP** — Manufacturer's Suggested Retail Price / Minimum Advertised Price
- **Wholesale price** — what you pay (typically 40–60% of MSRP)
- **Minimum order quantity (MOQ)** — first order is usually the largest
- **Payment terms** — Net 15, Net 30, or pre-pay
- **Shipping terms** — FOB origin (you pay shipping) or delivered
- **Return policy** — most don't accept returns from resellers

### Step 5: Test order

Place a small test order:
- 25–100 units of the cheapest SKU
- Receive, inspect, ship to FBA
- Track sales for 30 days
- If the math works: scale up. If not: pivot to a different SKU or supplier.

## What good wholesale looks like

A typical successful wholesale relationship:

- 5–20 SKUs you reorder monthly
- Per-SKU profit: \$3–\$15 net
- Per-SKU velocity: 30–200 units/month
- Per-SKU ROI: 20–35%
- 30-day cash cycle: order Monday → receive Friday → list following Monday → sales over 4–6 weeks → reorder

Run 3–5 of these and you're at \$30K–\$100K/month revenue with predictable monthly orders.

## What bad wholesale looks like

Anti-patterns to walk away from:

### Pattern 1: MAP violators flooding the listing
Your "exclusive" wholesale brand has 30 sellers all undercutting each other. The brand isn't enforcing MAP. Buy Box price has tanked.

**Walk:** the brand allows price erosion; your margin will dilute over time.

### Pattern 2: "Authorized" but really gray market
Brand's website says "authorized only" but they sell to anyone with money. Brand owner can later file inauthentic complaints if they decide your channel is unauthorized.

**Walk:** verify with the brand directly that you're documented as authorized.

### Pattern 3: Single-SKU dependency
You source 95% of revenue from one wholesale SKU. Then the brand discontinues it.

**Mitigation:** diversify. No single SKU >25% of revenue.

### Pattern 4: Net-90 terms
You order \$50K of inventory but pay 90 days later. By the time payment is due, you've sold most → cash flow works.

But if velocity drops, you're holding inventory + a payment due. **Net-90 amplifies risk.** Be cautious until you've proven velocity.

## Distributor vs direct

Two paths:

### Direct from brand
- Lower per-unit cost (40–55% of MSRP)
- Higher MOQ (often \$5K+)
- Brand controls authorization
- Best for established sellers

### Through distributor
- Higher per-unit cost (50–70% of MSRP)
- Lower MOQ (often \$500+)
- Distributor handles relationships
- Best for new wholesale sourcers

A typical first wholesale path: distributor → prove yourself → open direct accounts with target brands.

## Wholesale sourcing math

How to evaluate a wholesale opportunity:

```
1. Pull Amazon Buy Box price + 90-day average
2. Estimate fees (Referral + FBA + storage)
3. Subtract from Buy Box → "Net to seller" estimate
4. Ask wholesale: what's my cost?
5. Calculate ROI = (Net to seller - Cost) / Cost
6. Calculate Profit/unit = Net to seller - Cost
7. Estimate velocity from Keepa BSR drops + offer count

Decision criteria:
- ROI ≥ 15% (lower than RA because volume compensates)
- Profit/unit ≥ $3 (must be worth your time + risk)
- Velocity ≥ 30/month at current Buy Box
- ≤ 5 FBA sellers (to share Buy Box reasonably)
```

Test with 25 units, scale if numbers hold for 30 days.

## The MAP issue

MAP (Minimum Advertised Price) is the **lowest price the brand allows** anywhere online.

If brand sets MAP at \$30 and Amazon's Buy Box drops to \$28, the brand expects sellers to raise prices. But on Amazon, automated repricers race to the bottom, breaking MAP.

**MAP-protected brands** actively enforce by:
- Removing offending sellers' authorization
- Filing inauthentic complaints
- Demanding "show cause" letters

If you see a Brand Registered listing with stable Buy Box at MSRP, the brand enforces MAP. Stable Buy Box = good for you (margins protected). But you must respect MAP yourself or risk losing authorization.

**Don't chase Buy Box below MAP.** Even if your repricer wants to. Match MAP and accept the Buy Box share you can earn.

## Wholesale + Brand Registry combo

Some BoxedUp members reach a stage where they:
1. Establish wholesale relationships with smaller brands
2. Negotiate exclusive Amazon distribution rights ("you're our only Amazon seller")
3. Brand Registers the trademark (with brand's permission, sometimes via licensing)
4. Effectively become the brand's Amazon arm

This is a multi-year relationship game. Outcomes:
- 30–50% margins (vs 20% standard wholesale)
- Listing control (you own the listing)
- Sponsored Brand ads (brand-protected)
- Enterprise-scale orders (\$100K+ POs)

Few sellers reach this level. Those who do operate \$1M+ businesses.

## When wholesale is hard

Reasons why ~70% of attempted wholesale relationships fail:

- ❌ Brand requires "Amazon-approved seller" without a realistic path to qualify
- ❌ MOQ is too high (\$10K+ for first order — too much risk)
- ❌ MAP enforcement is lax → margins erode quickly
- ❌ Inventory turnover is slow → cash trapped
- ❌ Brand churns wholesale partners — relationship lasts 6 months then ends

Successful wholesale sourcers expect to apply to 20+ brands to find 3–5 productive relationships. Treat it as a long sales process, not a quick win.

## Wholesale tools

Past 3 wholesale relationships, you'll need:
- Automated reorder-prediction (built into many bookkeeping/inventory tools)
- **Bookskeep** or e-commerce CPA — multi-supplier accounting
- **Spreadsheet of brand contacts + status** — applications pending, approved, rejected, terminated
- **Proper inventory management** — track per-supplier P&L
