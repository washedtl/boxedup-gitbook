# Competition: FBA, FBM, Amazon-on-listing

🟡 **Intermediate.** Who else is selling on the listing — and how — determines whether you'll actually win sales. This page breaks down each competitor type and what each means for your odds.

## The Buy Box reality

Roughly **85% of all Amazon sales go through the Buy Box.** If you don't win it, you sell almost nothing.

Buy Box winners are determined by an Amazon algorithm that weighs:
- Fulfillment method (FBA > FBM)
- Price (lower = better, but not always)
- Seller performance metrics
- Stock level
- Account age + history
- Customer service quality (response time, ODR)

You don't get to see the algorithm directly. You see the result: who currently owns the Buy Box, and what price they hold it at.

## Competitor types

### Amazon (orange line in Keepa)

Amazon selling the listing themselves.

**What it means for you:**
- Amazon wins ~95% of Buy Box when in stock
- Your effective sales: nearly zero
- Your inventory will sit until Amazon goes OOS

**Decision:** if Amazon is currently selling, **don't source.** Period.

**Exception:** if Amazon's orange line shows in/out patterns (stockouts every 30–60 days), you can source for the gap windows. Risky — requires monitoring.

### FBA sellers (red on Keepa offer count)

Other resellers fulfilling via FBA.

**What it means for you:**
- You compete fairly with them via the Buy Box rotation
- Each FBA seller takes a roughly equal share over time
- Your share ≈ 1/(number of FBA sellers + you)

**Sweet spot:** 2–4 FBA sellers besides you. Enough that the listing is established but not so many that share is diluted.

**Decision:**
- 0 FBA: you might own 100% but listing might be too new/risky
- 1–2 FBA: you'll capture meaningful share
- 3–5 FBA: standard competition
- 6+ FBA: share gets thin, watch for race-to-bottom pricing
- 15+ FBA: skip; this listing is cooked

### FBM sellers (green on Keepa)

Resellers fulfilling themselves.

**What it means for you:**
- FBM rarely wins Buy Box vs your FBA offer
- They're mostly invisible to the Buy Box algorithm
- Don't worry about them unless...

**Watch for:**
- A single FBM at a much lower price → they'll eventually win Buy Box during your stockouts
- A high-volume FBM with great metrics → could be SFP (Seller Fulfilled Prime), threat to you
- FBM count rising rapidly → category may be saturating

In general: **FBM is competitive noise, not real competition.**

### SFP sellers (Seller Fulfilled Prime)

FBM sellers who maintain Prime SLAs and earn the Prime badge.

**What it means for you:**
- They compete with FBA on Buy Box
- They often have high-volume operations
- Their margins are different (no FBA fees but real shipping costs)

You can identify SFP by:
- Prime badge on FBM offer (rare, distinctive)
- Often signals an established 3PL operation

If a listing has 1+ SFP seller, expect:
- More aggressive Buy Box competition
- Possibly lower prices (SFP can sometimes underprice FBA when their shipping is cheap)

## The Buy Box rotation

When 2+ FBA sellers exist with similar prices, Amazon rotates Buy Box ownership over time. Roughly:

- Time-share basis: each seller gets some hours per day
- Skewed toward higher metrics (lower ODR, faster response)
- Rotation evens out over weeks

You can monitor your Buy Box win rate in Seller Central:
- Reports → Business Reports → "Buy Box Percentage"
- Healthy: 70–95% on listings where you should be competitive
- Below 50%: something's off (price too high, metric issue)

## Identifying who's on the listing

In Seller Central, on any listing you're selling:
- Click "Offer Details"
- See all sellers + their prices + fulfillment methods

For listings you haven't joined yet:
- Open the listing on Amazon as a customer
- Click "X new from \$Y.YY"
- See the full offer list

This is **mandatory pre-sourcing diligence.** Don't buy inventory without checking who you'll compete against.

## Hidden competition signals

### Signal 1: Recent offer-count spike
Keepa's "offer count" line spiked from 3 to 15 in the last 14 days = a deal-feed pile-on. The listing is cooked.

**Action:** skip. Even if math looks good now, Buy Box will tank within 30 days.

### Signal 2: Price war pattern
Buy Box bouncing 5–10% multiple times daily = aggressive repricing among existing sellers.

**Action:** caution. You'll be pulled into the price war, and your repricer might race to bottom.

### Signal 3: Single-seller manipulation
One seller has the listing alone, holding price high. You enter, they slash to maintain Buy Box share, you sit at high inventory.

**Action:** check seller-count history. If listing has been single-seller for months, expect this on entry.

### Signal 4: Same seller across many listings
You see "Brand Direct" or "Brand Authorized Reseller" on multiple listings. Brand is consolidating distribution.

**Action:** check if "Brand Direct" is the brand owner. If yes, brand is removing competitors → you're at IP risk if you compete.

### Signal 5: Authentic / suspicious mix
Some sellers sell authentic + counterfeit on same listing. Hard to detect; you'll see clues like:
- Wide price range (\$10 vs \$30 for "same" item)
- Mixed customer review quality
- Brand IP claim history

**Action:** if you suspect counterfeit, walk. Even authentic sellers get caught in IP wars.

## Competition by listing type

### Generic / unbranded
- Often many sellers
- Race to bottom on price
- No Brand Registry protection
- Margins thin but predictable

### Brand-Registered, brand owner selling
- Brand owner often wins Buy Box
- Other sellers reduced to gap-filling
- IP risk if you don't have authorization
- Often skip entirely

### Brand-Registered, brand NOT selling
- Other resellers compete normally
- Brand may file IP complaints periodically
- Source carefully with receipts/invoices

### Bundle / multi-pack listings
- Often single-seller (the creator)
- Less competition by design
- But low velocity per listing

## Calculating your competitive position

Quick framework before sourcing:

```
Your competitive position = 
  (Your fulfillment advantage) - (Their fulfillment disadvantage)
  + (Account age advantage) - (Account age disadvantage)
  + (Pricing flexibility) - (Their pricing rigidity)
  + (Inventory depth) - (Their stockout frequency)
```

If you're significantly behind on most factors: skip the listing.

## Strategy by competitor count

### Solo (you'd be only seller)
- Set your own price
- Risk: you're entering a listing for a reason — verify why nobody else is here
- Reward: 100% of Buy Box

### 2-3 FBA total (including you)
- Healthy competition
- Buy Box rotates fairly
- Most BoxedUp arbitrage happens here

### 4-7 FBA total
- Share dilutes but listing is "real" (real demand)
- Watch for repricer wars
- Margin pressure

### 8-15 FBA total
- Saturating
- Source only if velocity is huge AND price is stable
- Skip if either signal is missing

### 15+ FBA
- Cooked. Walk.

## Strategic posture

Your posture changes by competition:

### When you're alone
- Premium pricing OK
- Slow inventory turn fine if margin's high
- Watch for new entrants — they'll undercut on day 1
- Set Tempo / monitor on the listing for offer-count changes

### When in healthy competition
- Match Buy Box but don't undercut
- Don't enable aggressive repricing
- Maintain inventory (stockouts give competitors time to gain Buy Box share)

### When in a price war
- Don't engage — you'll lose
- Either: walk away, or hold prices and wait for competitors to clear inventory
- Consider removing inventory if war is severe

## What kills competitive position

Five things that drop you out of Buy Box rotation:

1. **Stockouts** — Amazon prefers in-stock sellers
2. **High ODR** — defects above 1% effectively block Buy Box
3. **Late shipment rate** (FBM only) — same effect
4. **New account** — first 30 days have lower Buy Box weight
5. **Tax/legal flags** — IP complaints suppress Buy Box

Maintaining metrics is competitive position, not just account-health protection.
