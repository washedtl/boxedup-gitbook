# The profit equation

🟢 → 🟡 — the formula. Memorize it. Every "is this a buy?" decision in this guide reduces to running these numbers.

## The full equation

```
Net Profit = Sell Price − COGS − Referral Fee − FBA Fee − Inbound Shipping − Misc
```

Each term, decoded:

| Term | What it is | Typical range |
|---|---|---|
| **Sell Price** | The Buy Box price (not "lowest new") | Use Keepa's 90-day Buy Box average |
| **COGS** | What you paid the store/supplier — including tax if non-exempt | Your receipt |
| **Referral Fee** | Amazon's commission, % of sell price | 8–15% (most categories: **15%**) |
| **FBA Fee** | Pick/pack/ship fee, by size & weight tier | \$3.22–\$10+ for standard; more for oversize |
| **Inbound Shipping** | What you pay UPS/Amazon Partnered Carrier to send to FBA | ~\$0.30–\$0.50/lb |
| **Misc** | Prep supplies (bags, labels, bubble), inbound placement fee, returns reserve | ~\$0.50–\$1.00/unit |

## A worked example

You find a toy at Walmart, clearance for **\$8.00**. Amazon Buy Box is **\$24.99**. It's a standard-size, 1.2 lb item.

```
Sell Price          = $24.99
- Referral (15%)    = -$3.75
- FBA fee (std 1lb) = -$3.86
- Inbound ship      = -$0.50
- Misc (prep+misc)  = -$0.75
- COGS              = -$8.00
─────────────────────────────
Net Profit          =  $8.13
ROI                 = 8.13 / 8.00  = 102%
Margin              = 8.13 / 24.99 = 32.5%
```

That's a **buy**. Anything over 30% ROI on RA with strong velocity is a yes.

## ROI vs Margin — pick the right one

These tell you different things:

- **ROI** = how hard your *cash* is working. Use this when comparing two flips.
- **Margin** = how thick your safety cushion is. Use this when deciding to walk if Amazon drops the price 10%.

**Same flip, two perspectives:**

| Flip | Cost | Sell | Net | ROI | Margin |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| A | \$8 | \$25 | \$8 | 100% | 32% |
| B | \$80 | \$120 | \$24 | 30% | 20% |

Which is "better"? Depends on:
- **Cash-constrained?** A wins (100% ROI, you can recycle cash 3× before B sells once)
- **Risk-averse?** B is safer if velocity is high — even if Amazon drops price 10%, B is still profitable

**The BoxedUp default:** prioritize ROI when you're sub-\$10K/mo. Prioritize margin when you're scaling and your cash has room.

## The "30/3/1" rule

If you remember nothing else, remember this rule of thumb for evaluating a flip:

> **30% ROI** minimum, **3 sales/month** minimum projected for you, **\$1 net** minimum per unit (otherwise your time isn't worth it).

For OA / wholesale, drop to **20% ROI** because the volume usually makes up for it. For replens and high-velocity SKUs, drop further to **15% ROI** because cash recycles fast.

## What people forget to subtract

Most beginners overestimate profit by 10–25% because they miss these:

### 1. Inbound shipping (the silent killer)

A 5lb item costs ~\$2.00 to ship to FBA. Beginners model \$0.

### 2. Returns reserve

About 5–10% of FBA orders get returned. You eat the FBA return fee + sometimes lose the unit (damaged in transit). Build in a **5% returns haircut** to your projected profit.

### 3. The new "inbound placement fee"

Amazon now charges if you ship to fewer warehouses than they want. Plan for **\$0.30–\$1.50/unit** depending on size, unless you split inventory across 4+ warehouses (which costs you in labor + boxes).

### 4. Sales tax on COGS

If you're in a state with no exemption, you paid tax on the receipt. That's part of COGS.

### 5. Storage fees

A unit sitting in FBA for 6 months in a category like Toys (Q4 storage premium) can rack up \$0.50–\$2.00 in storage. Build in a **storage haircut** for slow movers.

## The fee math gets harder for…

- **Oversize / heavy** items (FBA fee scales fast, \$15+ common for 5lb+)
- **Small & light** under \$10 (referral fee % is the same but FBA fee eats the unit)
- **Apparel** (15% referral, plus apparel tier FBA fees)
- **Books / Media** (special tier — FBA fees lower, but storage rules differ)
- **Hazmat** (longer prep, slower inbound, sometimes restricted)

When in doubt, **plug the actual ASIN into Amazon's [FBA Revenue Calculator](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/revcal)** before buying. Don't trust your gut on fees — Amazon's calculator is free and accurate.

## What "safe" margins look like

After ~200 flips, you'll have a feel for what numbers survive a price tank. The defaults:

| Category | Min ROI | Min Margin | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| RA fast-mover | 30% | 20% | Quick cash recycle absorbs price drops |
| RA slow-mover | 50% | 30% | Need cushion for storage + tank risk |
| OA (online) | 20% | 15% | Can re-source if price holds, lower volatility |
| Wholesale | 15% | 12% | Volume + relationship makes up for thin margins |
| Replen (proven) | 15% | 10% | History de-risks it |

## Putting it together — the 30-second deal scan

Standing in a Walmart aisle with phone in hand:

1. **Scan UPC** → SAS / BoxedUp tool fetches data
2. **Check ROI** — must be ≥ 30%
3. **Check Buy Box stability** in Keepa (last 90 days)
4. **Check FBA seller count** — ≤ 3 ideal, ≤ 5 acceptable
5. **Check BSR drops** — ≥ 6 in last 30 days
6. **Multiply estimated monthly sales × your share × net profit per unit** → is this worth the buy + prep time?
7. If all 6 check out → buy

After 30 days of doing this, it'll take you 5 seconds per scan.
