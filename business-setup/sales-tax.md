# Sales tax: nexus, marketplace facilitator, exemptions

🟡 **Intermediate.** Sales tax used to be a nightmare for resellers. After Marketplace Facilitator laws (2018+), Amazon handles 95% of it for you — but the remaining 5% trips up most members. Here's the framework.

> ⚠️ **Not tax advice.** Sales tax rules vary by state and change frequently. Confirm with a CPA before relying on this for filings.

## The good news first

Since 2019, every US state with sales tax has a **Marketplace Facilitator law**. Under these laws:

- Amazon collects sales tax from the buyer
- Amazon remits sales tax to the state on your behalf
- **You don't have to file sales tax returns for FBA sales in most states**

This was a game-changer. Pre-2019, sourcers needed sales tax permits in 30+ states. Now, often 0–2.

## What you still owe

You're not 100% off the hook. Three categories of tax remain your responsibility:

### 1. Income tax on profit
Federal + state income tax on your business profit (after costs). This isn't sales tax — it's normal income tax on your Schedule C / S-corp K-1. Filed annually with your tax return.

### 2. Use tax on items you bought tax-free
If you have a reseller certificate and bought items tax-free intending to resell, but you ended up consuming them (sold elsewhere, gave away, used personally), you owe **use tax** on those items at your home state's rate. Usually filed with your sales tax return.

### 3. Sales tax in your home state on direct sales
If you sell off-Amazon (your own website, eBay without their facilitator handling, in-person), you collect sales tax in your home state. This is rare for arbitrage sellers — most are 100% Amazon.

## The reseller certificate

**The reseller certificate is the most useful piece of paper in arbitrage.**

A reseller certificate (also called resale certificate, sales tax exemption certificate) lets you buy inventory **without paying sales tax** when your intent is to resell it. Sales tax gets collected from the end customer instead.

### Why it matters

Without a reseller cert: you pay sales tax at retail (~6–10% on every purchase). On a \$10,000/month sourcing operation in a 9% sales tax state, that's **\$900/month in unnecessary cost**.

With a reseller cert: you pay \$0 in sales tax on inventory purchases. Pure margin recovery.

### How to get one

Process varies by state:

1. **Get an EIN** (free, IRS.gov)
2. **Register for a state sales tax permit** (your state's Department of Revenue)
3. **Receive your reseller certificate** — usually mailed or emailed within 1–4 weeks
4. **Submit it to retailers** to claim tax exemption on purchases

States vary on:
- Whether the certificate is paper or digital
- Whether retailers accept your cert from another state (some are "Streamlined Sales Tax" states)
- Whether you need to renew annually

### Using it at retailers

Different retailers have different exemption flows:

#### Amazon Business
- Sign up for an Amazon Business account
- Submit your reseller certificate
- Future Amazon Business purchases are tax-free

#### Walmart Tax Exempt Program
- Apply at https://walmart.com/business/account/tax-exempt
- Submit your certificate
- Use the program ID at checkout (online + in-store)

#### Target
- Apply at Target.com → Tax Exempt → upload certificate
- Use the program at checkout (in-store: ask the cashier; online: select tax-exempt at checkout)

#### Home Depot, Lowes
- Their "Pro Xtra" or commercial accounts include tax exemption
- Apply via their commercial portals

#### Costco, Sam's Club
- Both accept reseller certs but require business membership

#### Smaller / local retailers
- Manual: present cert at register; cashier rings up tax-exempt
- Always carry a copy or digital photo of your cert

## Marketplace Facilitator — state by state

| State | MPF law? | What Amazon collects | Your obligation |
|---|---|---|---|
| All 45 sales-tax states + DC | ✅ Yes (since 2019) | Sales tax on FBA sales | Usually nothing for FBA-only sales |

A few states still have reporting quirks:

- **California, Texas** — sometimes ask for a "Sales tax permit" registration even though Amazon handles collection. Filing is informational ("I had no taxable sales because Amazon collected").
- **Washington** — has a "Business & Occupation tax" that's separate from sales tax. May apply to your gross sales.
- **Colorado** — has "retail delivery fee" on top of sales tax (\$0.27/order). Amazon handles for you.

If you're FBA-only in 1 home state and Amazon handles the rest, your annual sales tax compliance is typically:
- File 1 home-state sales tax return per year (often \$0 owed if all sales went through Amazon)
- Maintain reseller certificate for purchases
- That's it.

## The economic nexus issue (mostly resolved)

Pre-MPF laws, having "nexus" in a state (FBA inventory stored there) triggered registration requirements. Many sellers had to register in 20+ states because Amazon stored their inventory in warehouses across the country.

After MPF laws, this mostly vanished. But:

- **Some states still consider FBA inventory as nexus** for income tax purposes (separate from sales tax)
- **Income tax** can still require filings in multiple states for high-volume sellers
- **Most resellers below \$1M revenue** don't have multi-state income tax issues

If you're scaling past \$1M annual revenue, **hire a CPA who specializes in e-commerce.** Multi-state income tax compliance is real money.

## Common mistakes

### Mistake 1: Not getting a reseller cert
You're paying \$500–\$1500/year in unnecessary sales tax. Get the cert in your first 30 days.

### Mistake 2: Using cert for personal purchases
Big no. If you buy something tax-free under your cert and then keep it for yourself, you owe **use tax** on it. State auditors look for this.

### Mistake 3: Forgetting state-level filings
Even with Amazon collecting, your home state often wants an informational return. Missing it = \$50–\$500 penalty.

### Mistake 4: Trying to get reseller certs in multiple states
Your home state's cert is enough for most retailers. Some retailers want a cert from your home state plus the state where the purchase happens — but this is rare and usually solved by the multi-state forms (e.g., Streamlined Sales Tax).

### Mistake 5: Comingling personal + business at retailers
If you're at a Walmart and using your business tax-exempt code on a personal purchase (gifts, household items), that's tax fraud. Always use a separate cart/account for personal vs business.

## When to hire a CPA

For BoxedUp scale:

- **Under \$50K revenue/year**: TurboTax + maybe a one-time consultation. Sole prop or single-member LLC, no S-corp election. Total tax compliance: ~3 hours/year.
- **\$50K–\$250K revenue**: Hire a CPA for annual filings. ~\$500–\$1500/year. They handle Schedule C + state returns. Maybe S-corp election conversation.
- **\$250K–\$1M revenue**: Quarterly check-ins with CPA. Multi-state income tax considerations. Bookkeeping software essential.
- **\$1M+ revenue**: Specialist e-commerce CPA + bookkeeping team. Multi-state sales tax + income tax. Possibly business entity restructuring.

Find an e-commerce-specialist CPA via:
- Recommendations from `#resources` in Discord
- Reseller-focused accounting firms (Bookskeep, AccountingProse, e-Comm Tax Pros)
- Don't use a generic small-business CPA — they don't understand FBA inventory accounting

## A note on Amazon fees and tax

Two related but separate things:

- **Sales tax** = collected from the buyer, paid to the state. Amazon handles via MPF.
- **Amazon fees** (referral, FBA, storage) = costs to you, deductible business expenses.

Don't confuse them on your books. Sales tax should never appear in your "expense" column — it's not your money in the first place.

### The Walmart in-store workflow (specific)

Walmart's tax-exempt program is the most-asked-about. The exact flow:

1. Print your state-issued reseller certificate
2. **Go to Walmart customer service** (not the regular checkout — they can't process this)
3. Present:
   - Your reseller certificate
   - Government ID
   - EIN documentation (sometimes asked)
4. Customer service issues a **Walmart tax-exempt card** with your program ID
5. Save the card. **Always present it at checkout** for tax-exempt purchases.
6. The cashier rings the transaction tax-exempt.

⚠️ **Don't ask the cashier to figure this out.** They can't. Customer service is the only path. If customer service is busy, come back later — don't try to work around it.

## The 30-minute first-week setup

If you're new and want to get tax-compliant in one afternoon:

1. ✅ Form your LLC (or operate as sole prop with EIN)
2. ✅ Apply for state sales tax permit
3. ✅ Receive reseller certificate
4. ✅ Sign up for Amazon Business + tax-exempt status
5. ✅ Sign up for Walmart tax-exempt (in-person)
6. ✅ Sign up for Target tax-exempt (online)
7. ✅ Add Home Depot + Lowe's tax-exempt programs (if relevant to your sourcing)
8. ✅ Save the certificate as a PDF + photo on your phone for in-person use

After this, your sourcing tax cost drops to zero and your annual compliance is a single state return.
