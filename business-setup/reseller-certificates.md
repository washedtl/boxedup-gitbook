# Reseller certificates by state

🟡 **Intermediate.** A reseller certificate (also called a sales tax permit, resale certificate, or sales tax exemption certificate) lets you buy inventory tax-free when you intend to resell. It's the single highest-value piece of paper in arbitrage.

## Why the cert is critical

Without it, you pay sales tax (~6–10%) on every sourcing purchase. On a \$10,000/month sourcing operation in a 9% sales tax state, that's **\$900/month in unnecessary cost.**

With it, your sourcing tax cost is \$0. Pure margin recovery.

## Getting a cert: by state

The process varies by state. Below are common patterns.

### States with simple online registration
Most modern states. ~20 minutes online application.

- California
- Texas
- Florida
- New York
- Colorado
- Washington
- Most others

Visit your state's Department of Revenue website (or "Department of Taxation and Finance" / "Comptroller" depending on state). Look for "Sales Tax Permit" or "Sellers Permit" application.

### States that require additional forms
Some states have multi-step processes:
- New Jersey: requires NJ Form REG-1
- Pennsylvania: PA-100 form
- Massachusetts: separate sales/use tax registration

### States with no state sales tax (no cert needed)
- Alaska
- Delaware
- Montana
- New Hampshire
- Oregon

If you live in one of these, you don't need a state sales tax cert. But you'll still want EIN + LLC for the wholesale benefits.

### States with quirks

**California:** The CDTFA (California Department of Tax and Fee Administration) handles permits. There's also a California-specific form (BOE-230) for tax exemption when buying from CA-based suppliers.

**New York:** NY's "Resale Certificate" (Form ST-120) is what you fill out and provide to suppliers.

**Texas:** Texas's "Texas Sales and Use Tax Resale Certificate" (Form 01-339) is used. Texas is one of the most reseller-friendly states.

**Washington:** WA also has a Business and Occupation (B&O) tax that's separate from sales tax. Higher complexity for high-revenue sellers.

## Multi-state considerations

You likely only need a cert in your home state. Some states honor each other's certs (Streamlined Sales Tax compact). Others want their own form.

| Scenario | What cert do you need? |
|---|---|
| Live in WA, source from WA retailers | WA cert |
| Live in WA, source from CA retailer (online) | WA cert (CA doesn't tax out-of-state buyers) |
| Live in WA, sell to customers in 50 states | Just home state cert; Marketplace Facilitator handles tax collection |
| Have FBA inventory in 30 states | Just home state cert (Amazon's MPF handles tax) |

For most BoxedUp arbitrage sellers: **one cert in your home state covers everything.**

### Pre-MPF context (historical, mostly irrelevant now)

Before 2019, having FBA inventory in a state created "nexus" requiring registration. Sellers had to file in 30+ states. This was a nightmare.

Post-2019, Marketplace Facilitator laws moved sales tax collection to Amazon. Most sellers don't need multi-state registration anymore.

**Confirm with a CPA** before assuming this applies to your specific situation, especially if you sell off-Amazon.

## Streamlined Sales Tax (SST) states

24 states participate in the SST program, which means they accept a uniform reseller certificate format:

Alabama, Arkansas, Georgia, Indiana, Iowa, Kansas, Kentucky, Michigan, Minnesota, Nebraska, Nevada, New Jersey, North Carolina, North Dakota, Ohio, Oklahoma, Rhode Island, South Dakota, Tennessee, Utah, Vermont, Washington, West Virginia, Wisconsin, Wyoming

If you have a cert from any one of these states, suppliers in any other SST state should accept it (with a uniform multi-state form like SST-001).

Practical use: rare. Most resellers don't need multi-state coverage. But if you ever need to apply tax exemption at a supplier in a different SST state, mention this option.

## Submitting your cert to retailers

Different retailers have different programs. Common flow:

### Amazon Business
1. amazon.com/business → sign up
2. Use your business email + EIN
3. Tax-exempt application: submit reseller cert PDF
4. After approval (1–7 days), all Amazon Business purchases are tax-free

### Walmart Tax Exempt Program
1. walmart.com/business/account/tax-exempt
2. Submit reseller cert
3. Receive Walmart Tax Exempt ID
4. Use the ID at checkout (online + in-store)

### Target Tax Exempt
1. target.com/c/business → enroll
2. Submit reseller cert
3. Use Target Tax Exempt ID at checkout

### Lowe's, Home Depot
- Apply via their Pro/Commercial accounts
- Tax exemption included with commercial accounts

### Smaller retailers
- Manual process: present cert at register
- Cashier rings up tax-exempt sale

### Online retailers without programs
- Some allow at-checkout tax exemption (rare)
- Most don't — you pay tax, then file for refund (cumbersome)

## What "use tax" is

Important nuance: if you buy something tax-free using your reseller cert, but you end up consuming it (selling elsewhere, using personally, giving away), you owe **use tax** on it.

State auditors check for this. Pattern: high tax-exempt purchases + low sales = "where did the inventory go?"

If you have inventory shrinkage (theft, damage, personal use), file the use tax voluntarily. Penalties for unfiled use tax compound quickly.

## Renewing your cert

State certs typically expire:
- Annually (some states)
- Every 2–5 years (most)
- Sometimes never (a few)

Track expiration dates. Suppliers using your cert need a current copy:
- Amazon Business: re-upload annually
- Walmart Tax Exempt: same
- Major suppliers: same

If you let cert expire, suppliers will start charging tax again until you renew.

## Multi-LLC complications

If you operate multiple LLCs (different brands, different ventures):
- Each LLC needs its own EIN
- Each LLC needs its own cert (filed for that LLC)
- You can't use cert from LLC A to make tax-free purchases for LLC B

For arbitrage sellers, one LLC + one cert is usually plenty.

## Common cert mistakes

### Mistake 1: Using personal SSN instead of EIN
Cert filed under your name + SSN. Works, but exposes SSN to suppliers.

**Fix:** get EIN, refile cert under business name + EIN.

### Mistake 2: Mixing tax-exempt + personal purchases
You buy a TV "tax-free" using your cert, then use the TV at home.

**This is fraud.** State auditors check via tax-return reconciliation. Penalties include back taxes + 25%+ penalty.

**Fix:** strict separation. Personal purchases = pay tax. Business purchases = tax-exempt.

### Mistake 3: Letting suppliers use cert without authorization
Cert is for YOUR business. Don't share with friends, partners, family who have their own businesses. Each business gets its own cert.

### Mistake 4: Forgetting state tax filings
Even with MPF (Amazon collecting tax), most states require an informational return from sellers. Missing it = \$50–\$500 penalty.

**Fix:** file annually even if you owe \$0.

### Mistake 5: Out-of-state purchases without cert
You're in WA, sourcing online from a TX retailer. They charge TX sales tax even though you're tax-exempt.

**Fix:** present your WA cert. If retailer doesn't have a tax-exempt program, file for refund directly via WA's Department of Revenue (cumbersome but workable).

## Cert templates by state

States provide downloadable PDF templates of their resale certs. Common ones:

- **California:** Form BOE-230
- **New York:** ST-120
- **Texas:** 01-339
- **Florida:** DR-13
- **Pennsylvania:** Form REV-1220

Keep a digital copy of yours on hand:
- Phone (for in-person retailer use)
- Email-ready (to send to online suppliers)
- Cloud storage (Google Drive, Dropbox)

## Quick FAQ

### "I'm a new LLC; can I get a cert?"
Yes. Apply for state cert as soon as your LLC is formed. EIN is the prerequisite.

### "Can I get a cert without an LLC?"
Yes (sole prop). Use your SSN or EIN. Less professional appearance for suppliers but functionally identical.

### "Does my cert work in other countries?"
No. Each country has its own VAT/GST/tax structure. Sourcing internationally requires separate handling.

### "What if I get audited?"
You'll need:
- Cert documentation
- Records of tax-exempt purchases
- Proof of resale (Amazon sales reports, eBay reports, etc.)
- Use tax filings (if any)

If you've been disciplined, audits resolve cleanly. Sloppy records = back taxes + penalties.

### "Can I use a cert for non-Amazon sales?"
Yes. Reseller certs are for ANY purchase intended for resale, regardless of where you sell. If you sell on eBay, Walmart Marketplace, Mercury — same cert works.
