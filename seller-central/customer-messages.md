# Customer messages & A-to-Z claims

🟡 **Intermediate.** Customer messages are the slow leak that quietly tanks accounts. Late or wrong responses count as defects. Done well, they protect Account Health and build positive feedback. This page provides templates for the most common situations.

## The 24-hour rule

Amazon counts a customer message **unanswered for 24+ hours** as a defect. Five defects in 90 days = warning. Weekend doesn't matter.

**Response strategy:** check messages at minimum once daily, weekend included. Many sellers check 2–3× daily during active hours.

## The auto-response setup

Set this up on day 1 in Settings → Notifications → Buyer-Seller Messaging → Auto-response.

Default text:
```
Hi! Thanks for reaching out. I'll have a full response for you within 24 hours, often much sooner. If your question is urgent, please mark the message as "Important." 

Thanks for your patience!
```

This buys you the 24-hour window without "no response" defects.

## The 6 message types you'll see

### 1. "Where is my order?" (WISMO)

Most common. Customer ordered, hasn't received, wonders where it is.

**Template:**
```
Hi [Customer name],

Thanks for reaching out about your order [Order ID]. I checked the
status — Amazon shows it [shipped/in transit/delivered] as of [date].

[If in transit:]
The estimated delivery date is [date]. You can track real-time at:
[tracking link from Amazon order details]

[If delivered but customer says not received:]
The carrier shows delivery on [date] to [address]. Sometimes packages
are delivered to a slightly different spot — check with neighbors,
front porch corners, or any safe-drop location. If still not found
after 48 hours, please reply and I'll initiate a replacement or refund.

Thanks for your patience.

Best,
[Your name]
```

**Internal action:** if pattern of "WISMO → customer found it" repeats, no action needed. If pattern of "WISMO → never found" emerges, may indicate carrier theft or false claims.

### 2. Damage or defect complaint

Customer says product arrived damaged or doesn't work.

**Template (FBA):**
```
Hi [Customer name],

I'm sorry to hear about the [issue]. That's frustrating.

For FBA orders, Amazon handles refunds + replacements directly through
their A-to-Z Guarantee. The fastest resolution is to:

1. Go to Your Orders → [order link]
2. Click "Get product support" or "Return or replace items"
3. Select "Damaged" / "Doesn't work" as the reason
4. Choose refund or replacement

Amazon will process your request within minutes.

If for any reason you can't complete that, please let me know and I'll
contact Amazon on your behalf.

Best,
[Your name]
```

**Why this works:** redirects to Amazon's automated system (which auto-resolves), prevents A-to-Z claim filing against you specifically, demonstrates responsiveness.

### 3. Wrong item received

Customer says they received a different product.

**Template (FBA):**
```
Hi [Customer name],

Apologies for the mix-up. Wrong item is unusual but does happen at
the FBA fulfillment center.

The fastest fix:
1. Go to Your Orders → [order link]
2. Select "Item is the wrong product"
3. Choose refund or replacement

Amazon will issue immediate resolution.

If you can share photos of the item received vs the listing, I'll
also flag it internally so we can audit our inventory.

Sorry for the inconvenience.

Best,
[Your name]
```

**Internal action:** photograph + report to your prep team or Amazon. Wrong-item indicates inventory commingling or labeling failure.

### 4. Late delivery / Prime SLA missed

Customer says delivery was late.

**Template:**
```
Hi [Customer name],

Apologies that your order arrived later than expected. Delivery delays
are usually carrier-related and outside seller control, but I
understand the frustration.

For Prime delivery guarantees, Amazon offers automatic credit:
1. Go to Your Orders → [order link]
2. Click "Late delivery" 
3. Amazon will automatically credit a portion of your account

If your situation isn't covered there, please let me know and I'll
explore additional options.

Best,
[Your name]
```

### 5. Pre-purchase question

Customer asks a question before buying.

**Template:**
```
Hi [Customer name],

Great question. [Direct answer to their specific question.]

[Optional: include a relevant product detail or specification.]

Let me know if there's anything else I can help with.

Best,
[Your name]
```

Be helpful and specific. Pre-purchase responses convert browsers to buyers.

### 6. Negative feedback follow-up

Customer left negative feedback.

**Template:**
```
Hi [Customer name],

I just noticed your recent feedback about [order]. I'm sorry your
experience didn't meet expectations — that's not what I want for any
customer.

[If FBA-fulfilled and the issue was with the product:]
For an immediate refund or replacement, please go to Your Orders →
[order link] and select "Get product support."

[If the issue is something you can directly resolve:]
I'd love to make this right. [Specific offer based on issue: refund,
replacement, partial refund.]

If after we resolve this you feel comfortable updating your feedback,
that would be appreciated — but no pressure either way.

Thanks for taking the time to share your feedback.

Best,
[Your name]
```

**Note:** Amazon prohibits incentivizing review/feedback changes. The phrasing above is mild and acceptable: you fix the issue, you ask politely, no pressure.

## A-to-Z claims

A-to-Z Guarantee claims are buyer-protected complaints filed against your seller account. They're more serious than customer messages.

### What triggers an A-to-Z claim

- Item not received
- Item not as described
- Late delivery
- Refund not processed
- Seller unresponsive

### Why A-to-Z claims hurt

- Each filed claim counts toward ODR (>1% threshold)
- Amazon often sides with the customer by default
- Claim wins reduce your AHR (Account Health Rating)

### Preventing A-to-Z claims

The best defense: **proactive customer service.**

If a customer messages with a complaint, refund or replace **before** they file. A refund proactively granted = no A-to-Z claim. A delayed response = A-to-Z claim filed.

**Rule:** for any complaint under \$50, refund without questioning. The defect avoidance is worth more than the lost revenue.

### Responding to A-to-Z claims

Once filed, you have ~3 days to respond. Steps:

1. Open the claim in Performance → A-to-Z Claims
2. Read the customer's complaint carefully
3. Choose your response:
   - **Grant the refund** — no defect counted, claim closes
   - **Provide evidence the customer is wrong** — risky, often loses
   - **Ignore** — you lose by default, defect counted

In 90% of cases, **granting the refund is the right call.** Especially for low-value items.

### When to fight an A-to-Z claim

Only fight if:
- Order value > \$50
- You have iron-clad evidence (signed delivery confirmation, photo proof, customer's order history shows abuse)
- The customer is repeating false claims (some buyers claim "not received" on multiple orders to defraud)

For these cases, your evidence:
- Tracking with delivery confirmation + GPS
- Photo of item before shipping
- Communication logs showing the customer received the item
- Pattern of false claims by the same buyer

Submit clearly with timestamps.

### Chargebacks

A separate but related issue: customer disputes the charge with their credit card company.

- Counts as ODR defect
- Often automated; harder to dispute
- For Amazon FBA orders, Amazon usually handles the chargeback for you

For FBM orders that get chargebacks: respond promptly through the credit card dispute process with evidence.

## Buyer behavior patterns

After 100+ messages, you'll recognize patterns:

### The "I changed my mind" buyer
Wants to return for buyer's-remorse reasons. Refund without question; cheaper than a defect.

### The "broken item" repeat buyer
Suspicious — same customer claims multiple orders broken. Look at their order history. If pattern of claims, escalate to Amazon (they have anti-fraud teams).

### The genuinely-confused buyer
Doesn't understand the product. Friendly explanation often satisfies; saves a refund.

### The "demand response in 1 hour" buyer
High-strung. Amazon doesn't require 1-hour response; 24 hours is the standard. Don't reset expectations.

### The "compensate me extra" buyer
Wants more than refund. Decline politely. "I can offer a refund per Amazon's standard policy. Beyond that is outside what I can do."

## Message metrics

In Performance → Customer Service Performance, you'll see:
- **Late Response Rate** — % of messages responded to in >24 hours
- **CSDR (Customer Service Dissatisfaction Rate)** — % of responses Amazon flagged as unhelpful

Targets:
- Late Response Rate: <10%
- CSDR: <25%

If either climbs, audit your message responses. Common causes:
- Auto-responses that don't actually answer (low CSDR)
- Weekend response gaps (high Late Response Rate)
- Argumentative tone (high CSDR)

## Tools for message management

For high-volume sellers:

### Amazon's native message system
- Free, works
- No automation beyond auto-response
- Adequate for <50 messages/week

### FeedbackWhiz / SellerLabs
- Email automation, pre-built templates
- ~\$50–\$200/month
- Worth it past 100 messages/week

### Custom support tools / VAs
- Above 200 messages/week, hire a VA to respond
- Train them with your templates
- Cost: \$5–\$15/hour for offshore VAs

## Tone guidelines

Across all message types:

✅ **Friendly but professional**
✅ **Specific** (use customer name, order ID, product)
✅ **Solution-oriented** (focus on fixing the issue)
✅ **Humble** (apologize even when not at fault)
✅ **Brief** (3–5 sentences max for routine issues)

❌ **Defensive** (never argue with the customer publicly)
❌ **Robotic** (don't sound copy-paste; personalize)
❌ **Over-apologetic** (one apology per message; not 3)
❌ **Long** (customers don't read 5-paragraph responses)

## Documentation

For every message:
- Note in your records: order ID, customer name, issue, your response, resolution
- For escalated issues (A-to-Z, chargebacks): save full message history

This protects you in:
- Future Amazon audits
- IP / inauthentic complaint defense
- Multi-account / fraud investigations
